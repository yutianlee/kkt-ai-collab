\qquad

W'(\zeta_c)=a_cA'(\zeta_c)+b_cB_A'(\zeta_c),
$$

where the prime denotes differentiation with respect to $\zeta$. Put

$$
\mathfrak C_c=(a_c^2+b_c^2)^{1/2}.
$$

Then

$$
|W(\zeta_1)|
\le
\mathfrak m(\zeta_1)\mathfrak C_c
\exp\left(
\pi
\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta_1}
\mathfrak m(\xi)^2|\Psi(\xi)|\,d\xi
\right).
$$

**Proof sketch.**

Let

$$
\mathsf A(\zeta)=
\begin{pmatrix}
A(\zeta)&B_A(\zeta)\\
A'(\zeta)&B_A'(\zeta)
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

The Airy Wronskian has absolute value $1/\pi$. Writing

$$
(W,W')^T=\mathsf A(\zeta)Z(\zeta),
$$

variation of constants gives

$$
Z'(\zeta)
=
\mathsf A(\zeta)^{-1}
\begin{pmatrix}
0\\
\Psi(\zeta)W(\zeta)
\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Using the Euclidean coefficient norm and the bound

$$
\|\mathsf A(\zeta)^{-1}(0,1)^T\|\,\|\mathsf A(\zeta)\|
\le
\pi\mathfrak m(\zeta)^2,
$$

one obtains

$$
\frac{d}{d\zeta}\|Z(\zeta)\|
\le
\pi\mathfrak m(\zeta)^2|\Psi(\zeta)|\|Z(\zeta)\|.
$$

Gronwall's inequality yields the stated estimate, and

$$
|W(\zeta_1)|\le \mathfrak m(\zeta_1)\|Z(\zeta_1)\|.
$$

Then

$$

$$

where

$$

=
\frac{\zeta K_{\tau\tau}}{4K^2}
-
\frac{5\zeta K_\tau^2}{16K^3}

\frac{5}{16\zeta^2}.

## Alternative 2: Riccati certificate for low degree and small $\alpha$

Define

$$
R(u)=p_B(u)\frac{H'(u)}{H(u)}.
$$

Then

$$
p_B(u)R'(u)+R(u)^2+K_B(u)=0.
$$

Near the endpoint,

$$
R(u)=\frac{\alpha}{2}+uW(u),
$$

and the regularized equation gives exact Taylor data. This route avoids Airy coefficient conditioning and can be used for low-degree interval certificates.

For $n=2$, one can combine:

## Alternative 3: Christoffel or reproducing-kernel bound

A sharp Christoffel-function inequality could imply pointwise bounds for weighted Jacobi polynomials. The obstacle is the constant: known Christoffel estimates generally give correct order but not the KKT sharp factor

$$
\left(
\frac{(n+1)B}{(n+\alpha+1)(B-\alpha)}
\right)^{1/4}.
$$

A meaningful next lemma would have to be very specific: a one-sided endpoint Christoffel bound at the first critical point, with the endpoint weight and gamma normalization built in. I do not see such a theorem ready for use.
