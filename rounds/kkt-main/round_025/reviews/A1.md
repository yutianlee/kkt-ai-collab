### A2

A2's most valuable contribution is the rational-coordinate Bessel relative-amplitude calculation. The exact normal form

$$
Y''+
\left(
\frac{\Lambda_B}{z}
+
\frac{1-\alpha^2}{4z^2}
+
\Delta Q(z)
\right)Y=0,
\qquad
z=\frac{Bu}{B-u},
$$

with

$$
\Delta Q(z)
=
-\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z}
-
\frac{\Delta_BB^2}{(B+z)^2}
$$

is a useful continuation of the Round 24 rational-Bessel track. A2's relative-amplitude identity is also structurally correct: if

$$
W_1(z)=\sqrt z\,J_\alpha(2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z})
$$

solves the Bessel core and $Y=hW_1$, then

$$
(W_1^2h')'+\Delta Q\,W_1^2h=0.
$$

Since $\Delta Q<0$, this may be written as

$$
(W_1^2h')'=|\Delta Q|W_1^2h.
$$

A2's elementary Volterra-kernel estimate is a useful fragment. Up to the first Bessel peak, the substitution $t=2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z}$ gives

$$
I(z)
=
\int_0^z
\frac{1}{W_1(y)^2}
\int_0^y W_1(x)^2\,dx\,dy
\le
\frac{T^4}{64\Lambda_B^2},
\qquad
T=2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z},
$$

provided $0<T\le j'_{\alpha,1}$ and $J_\alpha$ is positive and increasing on that interval. This is a real estimate, not just a scaling narrative.

A2's main overclaim is the word "proved" attached to the small-$\alpha$ theorem. The calculation gives a conditional Volterra estimate up to the Bessel reference peak. It does not yet prove the KKT first-lobe amplitude estimate because the true Jacobi critical point may occur after the Bessel reference peak, the Bessel denominator may approach zero before the integration endpoint, the gamma normalization $M_{n,\alpha,B}$ is still unbounded in theorem-level form, and the final KKT target margin has not been checked with explicit constants.
