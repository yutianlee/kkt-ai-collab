# Detailed reasoning

## Assumptions and notation
We work with the KKT normalised function

\[
g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)=
\Bigl(\frac{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(n+\alpha+\beta+1)}{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(n+\beta+1)}\Bigr)^{\!1/2}
\Bigl(\frac{1-x}{2}\Bigr)^{\!\alpha/2}
\Bigl(\frac{1+x}{2}\Bigr)^{\!\beta/2}
P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x),\qquad x\in[-1,1],
\]

\(n\in\mathbb N,\;\alpha,\beta\ge0\).  The target is

\[
|g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)|\le T_{n,\alpha,\beta},\qquad
T_{n,\alpha,\beta}=
\Bigl(\frac{(n+1)(n+\alpha+\beta+1)}{(n+\alpha+1)(n+\beta+1)}\Bigr)^{\!1/4}.
\]

After imports of central-contour, energy, small-exponent, and symmetry modules, the residual right-endpoint problem falls in

\[
n\ge1,\qquad \frac12<\alpha<\alpha_E(n),\qquad \beta\ge0,
\]

with \(\alpha_E(n)=\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}\).  Define

\[
s=\alpha+\beta,\qquad
B=n+\alpha+\beta+1=n+s+1, \qquad
\sigma=\frac{s}{s+2n}=\frac{B-n-1}{B+n-1},
\]

and the endpoint variable

\[
u=\frac{B(1-x)}{2},\qquad
H_{n,\alpha,\beta}(u)=g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}\Bigl(1-\frac{2u}{B}\Bigr).
\]

The cap after central clearance is

\[
0\le u\le u_\sigma=\frac{B(1-\sigma)}{2}=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n.
\]

## Exact algebra
### 1. Transform the Jacobi self-adjoint equation to the endpoint ODE
The Jacobi self-adjoint form for the normalised function \(g\) is

\[
(Ag')'+\frac{F}{A}\,g=0,\qquad A(x)=1-x^2,
\]

where

\[
F(x)=\kappa(1-x^2)-\frac14(d-sx)^2,\qquad
\kappa=nB+\frac{s}{2},\qquad d=\beta-\alpha .
\]

Now put \(x=1-\frac{2u}{B}\), so \(dx/du=-\frac2B\) and

\[
1-x=\frac{2u}{B},\qquad
1+x=2\Bigl(1-\frac{u}{B}\Bigr),\qquad
A(x)=\frac{4u}{B}\Bigl(1-\frac{u}{B}\Bigr).
\]

Compute \(g'=\frac{dg}{dx}=-\frac B2\,H'\) and \(A g'=-\frac B2 A H'\).  Then

\[
\frac{d}{dx}(Ag')=\frac{B^2}{4}\frac{d}{du}(A H').
\]

Since \(A=\frac4B\,p_B(u)\) with \(p_B(u)=u\bigl(1-\frac uB\bigr)\),

\[
\frac{B^2}{4}\frac{d}{du}\Bigl(\frac4B p_B\,H'\Bigr)
= B\,(p_B H')'.
\]

Hence

\[
(Ag')'=B\,(p_B H')'.
\]

The potential term becomes \(\frac{F}{A}g\); dividing by \(B\) yields the endpoint ODE

\[
\boxed{(p_B H')' + q_B H =0},
\]

with

\[
q_B(u)=\frac{1}{B}\frac{F}{A}
=c_B-\frac{(r_B u-\alpha)^2}{4u(1-u/B)},
\]

where

\[
r_B=\frac{s}{B}=1-\frac{n+1}{B},\qquad
c_B=\frac{\kappa}{B}=n+\frac12-\frac{n+1}{2B}.
\]

No factor \(B/2\) or any other spurious constant appears; the chain-rule calculation is exact.

**Status: certified.**

### 2. Quadratic product \(K_B\)
Define \(K_B(u)=p_B(u)q_B(u)\).  Multiplying,

\[
K_B(u)=c_B\,u\Bigl(1-\frac uB\Bigr)-\frac{(r_B u-\alpha)^2}{4}
=-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+\Lambda_B u-\Delta_B u^2,
\]

with

\[
\Lambda_B = c_B+\frac{\alpha r_B}{2},\qquad
\Delta_B = \frac{c_B}{B}+\frac{r_B^2}{4}.
\]

The sign and coefficients have been checked by expanding the square and collecting powers of \(u\).

**Status: certified.**

### 3. Cap-endpoint identity \(u_\sigma\)
The central boundary \(x=\sigma\) transforms as

\[
u_\sigma=\frac{B(1-\sigma)}{2}
=\frac{B}{2}\Bigl(1-\frac{s}{s+2n}\Bigr)
=\frac{B}{2}\cdot\frac{2n}{s+2n}
=\frac{nB}{s+2n}.
\]

Because \(s+2n=B+n-1\),

\[
u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n\qquad (n\ge1).
\]

**Status: certified.**

### 4. Monotonicity estimate \(K_B'(u)\ge\frac14\) on the cap
Since \(\Delta_B>0\), the quadratic \(K_B\) is concave, so \(K_B'\) is decreasing.  Hence on \([0,u_\sigma]\)

\[
K_B'(u)\ge K_B'(u_\sigma)=\Lambda_B-2\Delta_B u_\sigma.
\]

A direct rational simplification gives

\[
K_B'(u_\sigma)=\frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
=\frac{\alpha}{2}+\frac{\beta(n+1)}{2B}.
\]

For \(\alpha\ge\frac12\) this is at least \(\frac14\), with equality at the extreme \(\alpha=\frac12,\ \beta=0\).  Thus

\[
\boxed{K_B'(u)\ge\frac14\qquad(n\ge1,\ \alpha\ge\tfrac12,\ 0\le u\le u_\sigma)} .
\]

**Status: certified.**

### 5. Frobenius coefficient \(A_{n,\alpha,B}\) and Bessel normalisation \(M_{n,\alpha,B}\)
Near \(u=0\) the regular branch satisfies \(H(u)\sim A_{n,\alpha,B}\,u^{\alpha/2}\) with

\[
A_{n,\alpha,B}
=\frac{B^{-\alpha/2}}{\Gamma(\alpha+1)}
\sqrt{\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\,\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(n+1)\,\Gamma(B-\alpha)}} .
\]

This follows from the exact value of \(g\) at \(x=1\) and the limiting behaviour of the weight.  Matching the leading term of the Bessel candidate

\[
\mathcal B_{n,\alpha,B}(u)=M_{n,\alpha,B}\,J_\alpha\!\bigl(2\sqrt{\Lambda_B u}\bigr)
\]

gives

\[
M_{n,\alpha,B}
= A_{n,\alpha,B}\,\Gamma(\alpha+1)\,\Lambda_B^{-\alpha/2}
= \sqrt{\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\,\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(n+1)\,\Gamma(B-\alpha)}}\,
(B\Lambda_B)^{-\alpha/2}.
\]

No hidden constant has been lost; the factor \(\Gamma(\alpha+1)\) cancels correctly.

**Status: certified.**

### 6. Critical-point equation in \(x\) and in \(u\)
The first derivative of \(H\) vanishes when

\[
\frac{d}{dx}\bigl(w(x)P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)\bigr)=0,
\qquad w(x)=\bigl(\tfrac{1-x}{2}\bigr)^{\alpha/2}\bigl(\tfrac{1+x}{2}\bigr)^{\beta/2}.
\]

Computing the logarithmic derivative and using \(\frac{d}{dx}P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}=\frac B2\,P_{n-1}^{(\alpha+1,\beta+1)}\) yields

\[
\bigl(\beta(1-x)-\alpha(1+x)\bigr)P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)
+B\,(1-x^2)P_{n-1}^{(\alpha+1,\beta+1)}(x)=0.
\]

Substituting \(x=1-\frac{2u}{B}\) gives the \(u\)-form

\[
2(r_B u-\alpha)\,P_n\Bigl(1-\frac{2u}{B}\Bigr)
+4u\Bigl(1-\frac{u}{B}\Bigr)P_{n-1}^{(\alpha+1,\beta+1)}\Bigl(1-\frac{2u}{B}\Bigr)=0.
\]

**Status: certified.**

### 7. Liouville normal form -- corrected sign
For \(Y_u = p_B^{1/2}H\) one obtains

\[
Y_u''+\frac{K_B(u)+1/4}{p_B(u)^2}Y_u = 0.
\]

This follows from the general identity for \((p H')'+qH=0\) after the change \(Y=p^{1/2}H\), and the fact that for \(p_B(u)=u(1-u/B)\) the Schwarzian-type term simplifies to \(1/4\).  Versions using \(K_B-1/4\) are **rejected**.

**Status: certified.**

The dynamic oscillator
\[
H_{\tau\tau}+K_B(u(\tau))H=0,\qquad \tau=\log\frac{u}{B-u},
\]
is also certified.

## Theorem-dependency
The endpoint-cap reduction itself uses only elementary algebra and calculus within the normalised Jacobi ODE. No external theorem is invoked beyond the definitions.  The global modules (central contour, energy, small-exponent) are imported but are not part of this audit.  For the later amplitude analysis, the following external dependencies are required:

* **Landau (2000):** monotonicity of the Bessel maximum to reduce \(\sup_{\nu\ge1/2,\,t\ge0}|J_\nu(t)|\) to the half-order case.  The precise statement "\(\sup_x|J_\nu(x)|\) strictly decreases with \(\nu>0\)" must be quoted exactly.
* **Wendel (1948) or Gautschi/Kershaw:** gamma-ratio inequalities needed for the \(n=1\) certificate and for the general gamma envelope of \(M_{n,\alpha,B}\).
* **Dunster--Gil--Segura (2020/2019) or Olver:** simple-turning-point error bounds for the Langer/Airy track.  The KKT residual \(\Psi_B\) must be mapped to the theorem's hypotheses with explicit weight functions.
* **Binet--Robbins remainders:** for the rigorous finite-\(n\) gamma envelope.
None of these are needed for the algebraic identities above; they are listed so that A1/A2 can locate the exact theorem texts and verify the hypotheses when the analytic amplitude proof is attempted.

## Hidden assumptions and failure modes
* The derivation of the endpoint ODE assumes \(B>0\) (true for all non-negative parameters) and \(u\notin\{0,B\}\) (the cap avoids the second singularity because \(u\le u_\sigma\le n<B\) for \(n\ge1\)).
* The inequality \(K_B'(u)\ge\frac14\) depends on \(\alpha\ge\frac12\).  For the excluded range \(0\le\alpha<\frac12\) the right endpoint is already controlled by the small-exponent theorem, so this is not a logical gap.
* The Frobenius coefficient assumes \(\alpha>0\); the case \(\alpha=0\) (where \(H(0)>0\)) is covered by the elementary \(n=0\) or small-exponent modules and does not occur in the residual strip.
* The critical-point equation in the form with \(P_{n-1}^{(\alpha+1,\beta+1)}\) is valid for \(n\ge1\); \(n=0\) is treated separately.
* The compactification \(\theta=\frac{n+\alpha+1}{B}\in[0,1]\) is well-defined for all \(\beta\ge0\), including \(\beta\to\infty\) (\(\theta\to0\)).  At \(\theta=0\) the polynomial factors become standard Laguerre expressions; no \(0/0\) indeterminacy occurs if the compactified hypergeometric representation is used.
* The Liouville normal-form sign must be \(K_B+\frac14\), not \(K_B-\frac14\).  Using the wrong sign would misplace the turning point by a constant shift and can lead to incorrect Airy-zone analysis.

## Counterexample or obstruction search
I tested the formulas on three parameter samples:

| \((n,\alpha,\beta)\) | \(B\) | \(u_\sigma\) | \(K_B'(u_\sigma)\) | remark |
|----------------------|------|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| \((1,1,0)\) | 3 | 1 | 1/2 | \(>1/4\) |
| \((1,\frac12,0)\) | 2.5 | 1 | 1/4 | boundary case, equality holds |
| \((2,\frac12,0)\) | 3.5 | 7/4.5≈1.555 | ≈0.2511 | \(>1/4\) |

No contradiction was found.  The case \(n=1,\alpha=1/2,\beta=0\) gives exactly \(1/4\), confirming that the bound is sharp in the residual strip.  The degenerate case \(\alpha=\beta=0\) lies outside the strip because \(\alpha>1/2\) is required, so the vanishing of \(K_B'(u_\sigma)\) in that fully degenerate situation does not affect the proof.

## Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration
* **Rational-coordinate Bessel track:** The algebra of the rational variable \(z=Bu/(B-u)\) yields a regular residual \(\Delta Q(z)=-\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z}-\frac{\Delta_B B^2}{(B+z)^2}\).  This is algebraically verified; the amplitude problem, however, remains open.  The relative-amplitude identity \((W_1^2h')'=|\Delta Q|W_1^2h\) is a promising candidate for a sign-definite Volterra bound, but it requires rigorous control of the zero-free interval of the Bessel function and a detailed tail estimate.  In my exploration I could not find an obstruction that would kill the route; the main difficulty is obtaining explicit constants for the variation integral when \(\alpha=cn\).  This route is worth pursuing but should be treated as a secondary track.
* **Riccati equation:** The substitution \(v(u)=p_B(u)H'/H\) leads to \(p_B v_u+v^2+K_B=0\) with regular Taylor data.  For low degrees this could replace the Langer analysis entirely if a validated ODE solver is available.  The algebra is correct and may be useful for \(n=1,2\).
* **Airy relative-amplitude identity:** The transformation \(W=\mathfrak C_-\operatorname{Ai}(-\zeta)y\) yields \((\operatorname{Ai}^2 y')'=\Psi_B\operatorname{Ai}^2 y\).  This is exact but does not by itself bound the amplitude unless \(\Psi_B\) has a definite sign or a small absolute integral.  The identity is algebraically sound and could be combined with an integration-by-parts argument; it remains an exploratory tool.
* **Krasikov-style higher-order Sonin envelopes:** These would enlarge the class of monotone functionals, potentially covering the turning layer.  I did not find a ready-made functional that works for the endpoint oscillator; constructing one is a substantial analytic task and is not recommended until the main Langer track is exhausted.

# Potential gaps
* The Frobenius coefficient derivation uses the limit \(u\to0\); for \(\alpha=0\) the regular branch does not vanish, but \(\alpha=0\) is outside the residual strip.  The derivation is rigorous for \(\alpha>0\).
* The compactified hypergeometric formula and its stable evaluation at the Laguerre face \(\theta=0\) is algebraically verified, but any interval-arithmetic implementation must carefully treat the products.
* The Langer residual removable value is given as \(\Psi_B(0)=\frac{10\gamma K_{\tau\tau\tau}-9K_{\tau\tau}^2}{140\gamma^{8/3}}\); it is plausible but a CAS Laurent-expansion should be archived to confirm cancellation of singular terms.

# Useful lemmas
I provide a concise summary of the certified identities.

**Lemma 1 (Endpoint ODE).** \( (p_B H')' + q_B H =0 \) with
\( p_B(u)=u\bigl(1-\frac uB\bigr) \) and
\( q_B(u)=c_B-\frac{(r_B u-\alpha)^2}{4u(1-u/B)} \), where
\( r_B=1-\frac{n+1}{B} \), \( c_B=n+\frac12-\frac{n+1}{2B} \).

**Lemma 2 (Cap length).** \(u_\sigma = \frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n\) for \(n\ge1\).

**Lemma 3 (Quadratic Sonin product).** \(K_B = p_B q_B = -\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+\Lambda_B u-\Delta_B u^2\) with
\( \Lambda_B = c_B+\frac{\alpha r_B}{2},\ \Delta_B = \frac{c_B}{B}+\frac{r_B^2}{4}\).

**Lemma 4 (Cap monotonicity).** For \(\alpha\ge\frac12\) and \(0\le u\le u_\sigma\), \(K_B'(u)\ge\frac14\).

**Lemma 5 (Dynamic oscillator).** \(H_{\tau\tau}+K_B H=0\) with \(\tau=\log\frac{u}{B-u}\).

**Lemma 6 (Critical-point equation).** In \(u\)-coordinates, at a critical point,
\[
2(r_B u-\alpha)P_n\bigl(1-\tfrac{2u}{B}\bigr)+4u\bigl(1-\tfrac uB\bigr)P_{n-1}^{(\alpha+1,\beta+1)}\bigl(1-\tfrac{2u}{B}\bigr)=0.
\]

**Lemma 7 (Frobenius coefficient).** For \(\alpha>0\), \(H(u)\sim A_{n,\alpha,B}u^{\alpha/2}\) with
\( A_{n,\alpha,B}= \frac{B^{-\alpha/2}}{\Gamma(\alpha+1)}\sqrt{\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(B-\alpha)}}.\)

**Lemma 8 (Bessel normalisation).** \(M_{n,\alpha,B}= \sqrt{\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(B-\alpha)}}\,(B\Lambda_B)^{-\alpha/2}\).

**Lemma 9 (Correct Liouville normal form).** \(Y_u'' + \frac{K_B(u)+1/4}{p_B(u)^2}Y_u =0\) where \(Y_u=p_B^{1/2}H\).  (Versions with \(K_B-1/4\) are rejected.)

**Lemma 10 (Rational-coordinate ODE).** With \(v=\frac{Bu}{B-u}\) and \(H_v\) as usual,
\( (v H_v')'+\widehat q_B(v)H=0,\quad \widehat q_B(v)=\frac{\kappa B}{(B+v)^2}-\frac{(\beta v-\alpha B)^2}{4v(B+v)^2},\)
and \(v\widehat q_B(v)=K_B(u(v))\).

**Lemma 11 (Langer removable value -- pending CAS).** At a simple turning point \(u_0\),
\( \Psi_B(0)=\frac{10\gamma K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau_0)-9K_{\tau\tau}(\tau_0)^2}{140\gamma^{8/3}},\ \gamma=K_\tau(\tau_0).\)
