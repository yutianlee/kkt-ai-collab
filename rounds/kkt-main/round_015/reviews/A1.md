Claims that look correct:

1. **Dynamic oscillator.** From $(p_BH')'+q_BH=0$ and $d\tau/du=1/p_B(u)$, one gets

$$
H_\tau=p_BH',
\qquad
H_{\tau\tau}=p_B(p_BH')'=-p_Bq_BH=-K_BH.
$$

Thus

$$
H_{\tau\tau}+K_B(u(\tau))H=0.
$$

This is certified.

2. **Prufer equations on $K_B>0$.** With

$$
H=R K_B^{-1/4}\sin\phi,
\qquad
H_\tau=R K_B^{1/4}\cos\phi,
$$

A3's signs are correct:

$$
\frac{R_\tau}{R}
=
-\frac{K_{B,\tau}}{4K_B}\cos 2\phi,
\qquad
\phi_\tau
=
\sqrt{K_B}
+
\frac{K_{B,\tau}}{4K_B}\sin 2\phi.
$$

This is exact only in the allowed zone $K_B>0$.

3. **Airy scale at the original turning point.** If $u_0$ is the first zero of $K_B$, then

$$
K_B(u(\tau))
=
K_{B,\tau}(u_0)(\tau-\tau_0)+O((\tau-\tau_0)^2),
$$

where

$$
K_{B,\tau}(u_0)=p_B(u_0)K_B'(u_0).
$$

The natural local Airy scale is therefore

$$
\zeta=(p_B(u_0)K_B'(u_0))^{1/3}(\tau-\tau_0).
$$

4. **Liouville normal forms.** A3's simplification

$$
Y_u=p_B^{1/2}H
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
Y_u''+\frac{K_B(u)+1/4}{p_B(u)^2}Y_u=0
$$

is correct. Likewise, in the rational coordinate $v=Bu/(B-u)$,

$$
Y_v=v^{1/2}H
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
Y_v''+\frac{K_B(u(v))+1/4}{v^2}Y_v=0.
$$

The distinction between the Sonin/Sturm turning point $K_B=0$ and the Liouville-normal turning point $K_B=-1/4$ is important and should be preserved.

5. **Compactified hypergeometric factor.** For

$$
\theta=\frac{n+\alpha+1}{B},
$$

A3/A4 correctly write

$$
\frac{(B)_k}{B^k}
=
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}\left(1+\frac{j\theta}{n+\alpha+1}\right).
$$

Therefore

$$
P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)
=
\frac{(\alpha+1)_n}{n!}
\sum_{k=0}^n
\frac{(-n)_k}{(\alpha+1)_k k!}
\left[
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}\left(1+\frac{j\theta}{n+\alpha+1}\right)
\right]
u^k.
$$

This is continuous at $\theta=0$ and gives the Laguerre face.

6. **The $n=1$ critical equation.** A4's derivation is correct. Since

$$
P_1^{(\alpha,\beta)}\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)=\alpha+1-u,
$$

the positive critical points of

$$
H_1(u)
=
C\left(\frac{u}{B}\right)^{\alpha/2}
\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right)^{\beta/2}
(\alpha+1-u)
$$

satisfy

$$
(\alpha+\beta+2)u^2
-
\left[\alpha(B+\alpha+1)+\beta(\alpha+1)+2B\right]u
+
\alpha B(\alpha+1)
=
0.
$$

7. **Bessel maximum dependency.** Landau's theorem appears to supply the missing monotonicity of $\sup_x|J_\nu(x)|$ in $\nu$. For $\nu=1/2$,

$$
J_{1/2}(t)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi t}}\sin t,
$$

and the first positive maximum solves

$$
\tan t=2t.
$$

Numerically,

$$
t_1=1.1655611852072113\ldots,
\qquad
J_{1/2}(t_1)=0.6791921047069769\ldots.
$$

Together with Landau, this supports

$$
\sup_{\nu\ge1/2,t\ge0}|J_\nu(t)|<0.680.
$$

This fact is now citable, but any proof using it must still track the exact Bessel/Airy normalization and error terms.

with explicit constants after integrating the error kernel through the whole first lobe. The local residual size does not by itself imply a global $O(1/n)$ amplitude error.

Possible errors or hidden assumptions:

1. **A4's no-turning-point sign error.** In the residual strip $\alpha>0$,

$$
K_B(0)=-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}<0.
$$

Therefore, if $K_B$ has no zero in $[0,u_\sigma]$, then $K_B<0$ throughout the cap, not $K_B>0$. The conclusion that the maximum is pushed to $u_\sigma$ may still follow from forbidden-zone ascent, but the stated reason is wrong.

2. **A4's worst-case turning-point estimate is wrong.** For $\beta=0$ and $\alpha\approx n$, A4 estimates

$$
\Lambda_B\approx\frac{3n}{2},
\qquad
\Delta_B\approx\frac14,
$$

which gives roots

$$
u\approx (3\pm\sqrt8)n.
$$

The lower root is

$$
(3-\sqrt8)n\approx0.1716n,
$$

which is **inside** the cap

$$
u_\sigma\approx \frac{2n}{3}.
$$

Thus A4's statement that this scaling may push the turning point outside the cap and trivialize the worst case is not supported. The hard first-lobe problem persists in this regime.

3. **A3's boundary table has the same sign issue.** A3 says that if there is no zero of $K_B$ in the cap, then $K_B>0$ everywhere. That is impossible for residual $\alpha>0$ because $K_B(0)<0$. Correct statement: no zero in the cap means the entire cap is forbidden, $K_B<0$, and the forbidden-zone ascent lemma controls it up to the central boundary.

4. **A2 overstates coordinate-invariance consequences.** The fact that $u$ and $v=Bu/(B-u)$ are Mobius-related and have zero Schwarzian confirms covariance of the Liouville normal form. It does not imply that every practical error functional or every chosen approximation basis is identical. The statement should be recorded as "no free Schwarzian improvement from $v$," not as a complete equivalence of all numerical or analytic estimates.

5. **A2's $O(n^{-4/3})$ residual-to-$O(n^{-1})$ inference is not justified.** Local pointwise smallness near the turning point must be integrated against Airy/Olver kernels over the relevant contour. A smaller local exponent does not automatically beat the KKT slack globally.

6. **A4's "$M\le1$ in the transition strip" is not proved.** Even if the leading entropy exponent is negative for fixed $c=\alpha/n>0$, the small-$c$ and finite-$n$ transition can still have $M>1$. The next round must find the actual maximum of $M$ numerically and then prove an analytic envelope.

7. **Endpoint and central-boundary closure require closed-boundary handling.** Several reports say "controlled by the central contour at $u_\sigma$." If the central theorem is stated only for $|x|<\sigma$, the proof must add a continuity/limit argument or a slightly overlapping clearance region. This is minor but must be explicit in the final theorem.
