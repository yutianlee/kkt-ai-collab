## Claims that look correct (algebraically certified or directionally sound)

1. **Exact affine endpoint ODE and cap reduction** (A1). The transformation from the Jacobi equation to

$$
   (p_B H')' + q_B H = 0
$$

   with \(p_B(u) = u(1 - u/B)\) and the stated \(q_B\) is identical to the earlier audited formulas (Rounds 12--14). The cap boundary \(u_\sigma = nB/(B+n-1) \le n\) and the product \(K_B = p_B q_B = -\frac{\alpha^2}{4} + \Lambda_B u - \Delta_B u^2\) are correct.

2. **Quadratic structure and monotonicity** (A1). The identities

$$
   \Lambda_B = c_B + \frac{\alpha r_B}{2}, \qquad \Delta_B = \frac{c_B}{B} + \frac{r_B^2}{4},
$$

   and the derivative formula

$$
   K_B'(u_\sigma) = \frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
$$

   are reproduced correctly. The sharpened bound \(K_B'(u) \ge \alpha/2\) on the residual cap follows from the concavity of \(K_B\); it implies \(K_B'(u) \ge 1/4\) precisely when \(\alpha \ge 1/2\), consistent with the residual-strip hypothesis.

3. **Forbidden-zone ascent** (A1). Using \(W = p_B H'\) and the sign of \(-q_B H\) (with \(q_B < 0\) before the first zero of \(K_B\)) shows that for \(\alpha > 0\) the regular endpoint branch is positive and strictly increasing up to that zero. The reasoning is algebraically sound.

4. **Sonin ordering and first-lobe reduction** (A1). The identity

$$
   S_B'(u) = -\frac{K_B'(u)}{q_B(u)^2} H'(u)^2
$$

   holds on intervals where \(q_B > 0\); consequently local extrema after the turning point are non-increasing in amplitude. Combined with the forbidden-zone ascent, the cap problem reduces to bounding \(|H(u_1)|\) at the first critical point after the turning point, provided the central-region module controls the boundary \(u = u_\sigma\). The logical structure is valid.

5. **Exact dynamic oscillator** (A1, A2, A4). The substitution

$$
   \tau = \log\frac{u}{B-u}
$$

   converts the endpoint ODE into

$$
   H_{\tau\tau} + K_B(u(\tau)) H = 0,
$$

   without requiring a Schwarzian correction. This is an algebraic identity and a substantial simplification.

6. **Liouville normal form and Mobius invariance** (A2). The affine normal form

$$
   Q_u(u) = \frac{K_B(u) + 1/4}{p_B(u)^2}
$$

   and its rational-coordinate counterpart

$$
   Q_v(v) = \frac{K_B(u(v)) + 1/4}{v^2}
$$

   are correctly derived. The rational variable \(v = Bu/(B-u)\) is a Mobius transform of \(u\); therefore \(\{v, u\} \equiv 0\) and no new curvature correction is introduced. This closes a previously open audit question.

7. **Static Bessel drift warning** (A2). The residual potential \(\Delta Q\) (difference between the exact Liouville potential and a static Bessel-frequency model) leads heuristically to an integrated Volterra defect of order \(\mathcal{O}(n)\) in the \(\alpha = cn, \beta = 0\) scaling. The algebraic steps (expansion of \(\Delta Q\), integration over the first-lobe scale) are plausible and reinforce the warning that fixed-frequency Bessel majorants are too blunt for the transition strip.

8. **Compactified hypergeometric formula** (A4). The identity

$$
   \frac{(B)_k}{B^k} = \prod_{j=0}^{k-1}\Bigl(1 + \frac{j\theta}{n+\alpha+1}\Bigr)
$$

   is algebraically exact and removes the indeterminate form at \(\theta = 0\). This will be required for any interval-arithmetic verification.

9. **Quadratic critical point for \(n = 1\)** (A4). The derivation of the quadratic equation for \(u_1\) (using the explicit linear Jacobi polynomial) is certified and provides a rigorous base case.

10. **Gamma-ratio regime split** (A2, A4). Both agents note that the leading \(n\ln n\) terms cancel in the Stirling expansion of \(\ln M_{n,\alpha,B}\). The resulting exponent is negative (A4 computes \(f(c) < 0\) for \(c \in (0,1]\)). Thus, contrary to earlier fears, the normalisation constant does **not** explode in the \(\alpha = \mathcal{O}(n)\) strip. The direction of the conclusion is correct, though a rigorous inequality has not yet been supplied.

## Claims that need proof (the active gaps)

1. **First-lobe amplitude theorem** (all agents). The central open theorem:

   For \(n \ge 1\), \(\frac12 < \alpha < \alpha_E(n)\), \(\beta \ge 0\), let \(u_1\) be the first critical point after the first endpoint turning point; prove

   $$
   |H(u_1)| \le \left( \frac{(n+1)B}{(n+\alpha+1)(B-\alpha)} \right)^{1/4}.
   $$

   is not proved by any Round 15 contribution. A1's conditional statement reduces it to bounding \(\mathcal{A}_h, E_h, k_1\), but those constants remain unspecified.

2. **Uniform Langer--Szego residual bound** (A2). The claim that the integrated Olver variation integral scales as \(\mathcal{O}(n^{-4/3})\) and therefore comfortably absorbs the \(\mathcal{O}(1/n)\) KKT slack is only a heuristic scaling argument. A proof must supply:
   - a rigorous expansion of the Schwarzian \(\Psi(\zeta)\) with explicit remainder bounds,
   - a global bound for \(\int_{\zeta_0}^{\zeta_1} |\Psi(\zeta)|/|\zeta|^{1/2} d\zeta\) over the actual first lobe,
   - explicit constants valid for all \(\alpha\) in the residual strip.

3. **Piecewise Prufer handoff obstruction** (A2). The derivation of the \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) boundary term \(A(\tau_h) = 1/(8 z_h^{3/2})\) rests on the linear approximation \(K_B \approx k_0 (\tau-\tau_0)\) and neglects higher-order terms in both \(K_B\) and the Prufer drift. To elevate this from a heuristic warning to a proved obstruction, one would need a full error analysis showing that the remaining non-adiabatic integral and the connection-coefficient adjustment cannot compensate the boundary term under any physically admissible handoff scaling.

4. **Rigorous gamma-ratio bound** (A2, A4). Binet's second formula or Robbins-type bounds for the logarithmic gamma function provide a route to uniform one-sided estimates, but neither agent has carried out the required remainder tracking. The statement that \(M_{n,\alpha,B} \le 1 + C/(n+1)\) for \(\alpha \le \sqrt{n}\) and that it decays exponentially for \(\alpha = cn\) is at present a plausible asymptotic, not a certified inequality.

5. **Bessel maximum monotonicity** (A4). A4 assumes (without citation) that \(\sup_{t>0} J_\nu(t)\) is strictly decreasing for \(\nu \ge 1/2\). Before a global bound \(B_* < 0.680\) can be used, a precise theorem with stated hypotheses must be supplied. The local half-order computation certifies the bound only for \(\nu = 1/2\).

6. **Interval-arithmetic execution for \(n = 2\)** (A4). The plan mentions using interval Sturm sequences or the cubic formula to isolate critical points, but the concrete implementation--with box subdivisions, interval evaluation of the target function, and a proof of strict negativity on every box--has not been carried out.

7. **Central-contour boundary at \(u = u_\sigma\)** (A1). The endpoint-cap reduction assumes that the imported central module controls the closed boundary point \(x = \sigma\) (equivalently \(u = u_\sigma\)). If the central theorem is proved only on the open interval \(|x| < \sigma\), the proof needs either a uniform-continuity argument or an explicit limiting estimate. This gap is acknowledged but not filled.

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

- **A2's "Obstruction Evaluated" statements are too absolute.** The adiabatic boundary term and the conclusion that it "structurally and permanently limits piecewise Prufer--Airy approaches" are presented with a certainty that exceeds the assumptions. The error from the neglected quadratic curvature at \(z_h = \mathcal{O}(n^{2/3})\) is not quantified; a more refined analysis could show that the total drift remains \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) but might be offset by a compensating contribution from the Airy-layer connection coefficient. The warning should be recorded as a serious risk, not as a definitive falsification.

- **A2's Schwarzian residual scaling.** The estimate \(\Psi(0) \sim \mathcal{O}(n^{-4/3})\) relies on the assumption that the \(\tau\)-derivatives \(c_1, c_2, c_3\) all scale like \(\mathcal{O}(n^2)\). The exact rational expressions for these derivatives may contain cancellations or lower-order terms; a full symbolic expansion (perhaps with the aid of a computer algebra system) is required to confirm that the leading term indeed yields \(n^{-4/3}\) and not a larger residual.

- **A4's gamma-ratio derivation.** Although A4 correctly labels the claim as "Derived-under-assumptions," the detailed calculation uses "≈" and omits remainder terms. Until a rigorous Binet-type bound is applied, the statement that \(M_{n,\alpha,B}\) decays exponentially in the transition strip remains a plausible asymptotic, not a lemma.

- **A4's label "Static Bessel Drift Obstruction ... [PROVED]"** is too strong. The scaling argument using Volterra integrals is a heuristic; a proof would require explicit constants and a rigorous variation-of-constants comparison theorem. This claim should be downgraded to a calibrated warning.

- **Turning-point nomenclature.** Several agents still refer to "turning point" meaning \(K_B = 0\) (the original Sonin turning point), while the Liouville-normal turning point occurs at \(K_B = -1/4\). The dynamic oscillator route avoids confusion by working directly with \(K_B\), but any Langer transformation or Airy matching must define explicitly whether it matches at the original \(K_B=0\) or at the normal-form turning point. A2's Langer map uses \(K_B\), which is consistent, but the distinction must be kept explicit in the final theorem.

- **Range of \(K_B'(u)\) lower bound.** A1 correctly states \(K_B'(u) \ge \alpha/2\) on the cap, implying \(K_B'(u) \ge 1/4\) only when \(\alpha \ge 1/2\). A2 later references an older bound of \(1/4\) without always specifying the residual restriction; this lacks precision but is not an algebraic error.
