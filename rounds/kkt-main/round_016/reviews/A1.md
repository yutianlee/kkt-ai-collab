Possible errors or hidden assumptions:

1. **A3 and A4 appear to use the wrong sign in the affine/rational Liouville normal form.** For

$$
(pH')'+qH=0,\qquad K=pq,
$$

and $Y=p^{1/2}H$, direct calculation gives

$$
Y''+
\left(
\frac{q}{p}
+
\frac{p''}{2p}
-
\frac{(p')^2}{4p^2}
\right)Y=0.
$$

For

$$
p(u)=u\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right),
$$

one has

$$
\frac{p p''}{2}-\frac{(p')^2}{4}=-\frac14.
$$

Therefore the normal-form potential is

$$
\frac{K_B(u)-1/4}{p_B(u)^2},
$$

not

$$
\frac{K_B(u)+1/4}{p_B(u)^2}.
$$

Similarly, in the rational coordinate $v$, the corresponding normal form should involve $K_B(u(v))-1/4$, not $K_B(u(v))+1/4$. This sign does **not** affect the exact dynamic oscillator, but it does affect any Liouville-normal turning point discussion. The “normal-form turning point” is $K_B=1/4$, not $K_B=-1/4$, under the convention $Y=p^{1/2}H$.

2. **A4’s $n=2$ cubic has a coefficient error.** A3’s coefficient list appears correct. A4’s formula

$$
E_2=Bc_1(\alpha+4)+b_1B

is missing an $\alpha+2$ contribution. Expanding

$$
(\alpha(B-u)-\beta u)P_2(u)+2u(B-u)P_2'(u)

with

P_2(u)=A-b_1u+c_1u^2,\qquad
A=\frac{(\alpha+1)(\alpha+2)}2,\quad b_1=\alpha+2,\quad c_1=\frac{B+1}{2B}

$$
E_2=
\alpha(Bc_1+b_1)+\beta b_1+4Bc_1+2b_1,

equivalently A3’s displayed expression. A4’s $E_2$ is lower by $\alpha+2$ after using $B=\alpha+\beta+3$.
