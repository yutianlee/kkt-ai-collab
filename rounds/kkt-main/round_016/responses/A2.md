### 1. Derivation of the Exact Endpoint ODE

We begin with the standard hypergeometric differential equation for the Jacobi polynomials $y(x) = P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)$:
$$ (1-x^2)y'' + [\beta-\alpha - (\alpha+\beta+2)x]y' + n(n+\alpha+\beta+1)y = 0 $$

The KKT normalized function is defined as:
$$ g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x) = C_{n,\alpha,\beta} \left(\frac{1-x}{2}\right)^{\alpha/2} \left(\frac{1+x}{2}\right)^{\beta/2} y(x) $$
where the normalization constant is $C_{n,\alpha,\beta} = \sqrt{ \frac{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(n+\alpha+\beta+1)}{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(n+\beta+1)} }$.

Substituting $y(x)$ isolated from $g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)$ back into the hypergeometric differential equation and dividing through by the resulting geometric factors casts the system into standard self-adjoint Sturm-Liouville form:
$$ \frac{d}{dx} \left( (1-x^2) \frac{dg}{dx} \right) + \frac{F(x)}{1-x^2} g(x) = 0 $$
where the modified potential function $F(x)$ is given by:
$$ F(x) = \kappa (1-x^2) - \frac{1}{4}(\beta-\alpha - (\alpha+\beta)x)^2 $$
and the characteristic eigenvalue is $\kappa = n(n+\alpha+\beta+1) + \frac{\alpha+\beta}{2}$.

Let $s = \alpha+\beta$ and the fundamental scaling constant $B = n+s+1$.
We map the physical space to the right-endpoint cap coordinate $u = B(1-x)/2$. 
This implies the spatial relations:
$$ 1-x = \frac{2u}{B} $$
$$ 1+x = 2 - \frac{2u}{B} = 2\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right) $$
$$ 1-x^2 = (1-x)(1+x) = \frac{4u}{B}\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right) $$
The spatial differential operator transforms via the chain rule as $\frac{d}{dx} = -\frac{B}{2} \frac{d}{du}$. 

We translate the self-adjoint kinetic operator carefully:
$$ \frac{d}{dx} \left( (1-x^2) \frac{d}{dx} \right) = \left(-\frac{B}{2} \frac{d}{du}\right) \left( \frac{4u}{B}\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right) \left(-\frac{B}{2} \frac{d}{du}\right) \right) $$
The leading minus signs cancel, and the scalar constants multiply to $\frac{B}{2} \cdot \frac{4}{B} \cdot \frac{B}{2} = B$.
Thus, the kinetic operator simplifies precisely to:
$$ B \frac{d}{du} \left( u\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right) \frac{d}{du} \right) $$

Next, we translate the potential term $F(x(u)) (1-x(u)^2)^{-1} H(u)$, where $H(u) = g(x(u))$.
Evaluate the squared geometric term in $F(x)$ entirely in terms of $u$:
$$ (\beta-\alpha - s(1-2u/B))^2 = (\beta-\alpha - s + 2su/B)^2 $$
Since $s = \alpha+\beta$, we have $\beta-\alpha - s = \beta-\alpha - \alpha-\beta = -2\alpha$.
$$ (-2\alpha + 2su/B)^2 = 4(su/B - \alpha)^2 $$
Define the fractional constant $r_B = s/B = \frac{\alpha+\beta}{n+\alpha+\beta+1} = 1 - \frac{n+1}{B}$. The squared term is $4(r_B u - \alpha)^2$.
Thus, the potential numerator is:
$$ F(x(u)) = \kappa \frac{4u}{B}\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right) - (r_B u - \alpha)^2 $$
Dividing by the exact kinetic factor $1-x^2 = \frac{4u}{B}(1-u/B)$ yields the transformed potential:
$$ \frac{F(x(u))}{1-x(u)^2} = \frac{\kappa \frac{4u}{B}(1-u/B) - (r_B u - \alpha)^2}{\frac{4u}{B}(1-u/B)} = \kappa - \frac{B(r_B u - \alpha)^2}{4u(1-u/B)} $$
Factoring out $B$ to match the kinetic term:
$$ B \left[ \frac{\kappa}{B} - \frac{(r_B u - \alpha)^2}{4u(1-u/B)} \right] $$
Noting that $\frac{\kappa}{B} = \frac{n(B) + s/2}{B} = n + \frac{s}{2B}$. Since $r_B = s/B$, we define $c_B = n + \frac{r_B}{2} = n + \frac{1}{2} - \frac{n+1}{2B}$.
The bracketed expression is defined as $q_B(u)$.
Dividing the entire transformed differential equation by $B$, we obtain the certified affine endpoint ODE:
$$ \frac{d}{du} \left( p_B(u) \frac{dH}{du} \right) + q_B(u) H = 0 $$
where the kinetic polynomial is exactly $p_B(u) = u(1-u/B)$ and the potential function is exactly $q_B(u) = c_B - \frac{(r_B u - \alpha)^2}{4u(1-u/B)}$.
The invariant Sonin product is defined as $K_B(u) = p_B(u)q_B(u)$. Multiplying out the fractions:
$$ K_B(u) = u\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right) \left[ c_B - \frac{(r_B u - \alpha)^2}{4u(1-u/B)} \right] = c_B u\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right) - \frac{1}{4}(r_B u - \alpha)^2 $$
Expanding the binomial $- \frac{1}{4}(r_B^2 u^2 - 2\alpha r_B u + \alpha^2)$ and grouping terms by powers of $u$ establishes the explicitly concave quadratic form:
$$ K_B(u) = -\frac{\alpha^2}{4} + \left( c_B + \frac{\alpha r_B}{2} \right) u - \left( \frac{c_B}{B} + \frac{r_B^2}{4} \right) u^2 $$

$$ K_B(u) = -\frac{\alpha^2}{4} + \Lambda_B u - \Delta_B u^2 $$
