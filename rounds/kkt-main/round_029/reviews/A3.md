## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

### A1

- **Scaling factor at \(\theta=0\):** The reduction of the critical equation to the compactified cubic uses a factor \(\theta\) that vanishes at \(\theta=0\). For \(\theta>0\) the equivalence is fine, but the exposition must keep clear that the cubic \(C_{\alpha,\theta}\) is not directly the critical equation at the Laguerre face; A1 correctly provides a separate limiting cubic for \(\theta=0\). The potential confusion is only a presentation issue, not an algebraic error.

- **Bernstein coefficient archive:** The new subbox certificate is stated to rely on exact rational Bernstein coefficients, but the answer only shows extremal values. The permanent proof must include either a full coefficient table or a script that regenerates them. Without that, a reviewer cannot distinguish a genuine enclosure from a floating-point approximation.

- **Crude gamma bound for other boxes:** The bound \(\Gamma(B)/\Gamma(B-\alpha)\le B^\alpha\) becomes increasingly coarse as \(\alpha\) grows relative to \(B-\alpha\). In the new box it is sufficient, but for boxes near \(\theta=0\) (where \(\beta\to\infty\)) or \(\alpha\) large, it will likely fail to close the KKT margin. The algorithm must have a built-in refinement (e.g., Wendel/Gautschi with explicit remainders) when the crude bound fails.

- **Cap endpoint underestimate?** The bound \(u_\sigma\ge15/8\) on the new box is derived from the minimum of the compactified formula; that is correct because \(u_\sigma\) is decreasing in \(\theta\) and decreasing in \(\alpha\). The explicit lower bound is safe.

### A2

- **Phase-shift linearisation:** The estimate \(\delta\approx -J_\alpha'(T_1^*) / J_\alpha''(j'_{\alpha,1})\) assumes a first-order Taylor expansion of \(J_\alpha'(T_1^*)\) about \(j'_{\alpha,1}\) without remainder. If the phase shift is not small compared to the scale of the derivative change, higher-order terms matter. The derived threshold may be quantitatively wrong.

- **Asymptotic Bessel constants:** The numbers \(1.8557\) and \(0.8086\) come from the Olver/McMahon expansions and are valid in a limiting sense; for finite \(\alpha\) (which can be as low as \(1/2\) in the residual strip) the errors are not controlled. A rigorous finite-\(\alpha\) bound for the zero gap is needed.

- **\(M_Q\) constant:** As noted, the constant \(2.75\) is not yet proven. If the actual sup is smaller, the threshold may be less restrictive; if larger, the quotient method may fail earlier. This constant must be bounded rigorously before any conclusion about the method's range can be trusted.

- **Zero-safety within the cap:** A2 does not check whether the potentially dangerous Bessel zero \(j_{\alpha,1}\) in the \(T\) variable lies outside the cap endpoint \(u_\sigma\) in certain parameter ranges. If the cap itself ends before the zero becomes relevant, the quotient singularity is a moot point for the *endpoint cap* proof. This should be part of the delimitation: for a given box, either prove \(T_\sigma=2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z_\sigma}<j_{\alpha,1}\) or handle the singularity explicitly. The analysis as written does not cross-check this.

- **Positivity of \(J_\alpha(T_1^*)\) and the integral:** The implication \(J_\alpha'(T_1^*)<0\) assumes \(J_\alpha(T_1^*)>0\) and that the integral is positive. While these hold in the observed regime, a rigorous proof would need to verify that no sign change occurs before the critical point; this is plausible but not yet demonstrated in the formal output.
