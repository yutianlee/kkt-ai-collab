Claims that look correct:

1. The rational-coordinate normal form and residual from A2 look algebraically correct. The same residual has been repeatedly identified in earlier rounds, and the Round 29 expression matches the established form

$$
\Delta Q(z)=
-\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z}
-\frac{\Delta_BB^2}{(B+z)^2}.
$$

The residual is negative, regular at $z=0$, and not pointwise small in general. Any smallness must come from the Volterra kernel and from restricting the Bessel argument interval.

2. A2's pre-peak Volterra estimate is correct in its stated domain. If

$$
T=2\sqrt{\Lambda_Bz}\le j'_{\alpha,1},
$$

and $W_1>0$, then monotonicity of $J_\alpha$ up to its first positive derivative zero gives

$$

\exp\left(
M_Q\frac{T^4}{64\Lambda_B^2}
\right),
$$

provided the Frobenius initialization is

$$
h(0)=M_{n,\alpha,B}.
$$

This should be added only as a conditional lemma with the explicit restriction $T\le j'_{\alpha,1}$.

3. A2's critical-point cancellation is correct as an algebraic identity. At a true critical point of $H$, the equation

$$
Y'=\frac{1}{2z}Y
$$

cancels the corresponding term in $W_1'/W_1$ and yields

$$
h'(z_1^*)
=
-h(z_1^*)\frac{2\Lambda_B}{T_1^*}
\frac{J_\alpha'(T_1^*)}{J_\alpha(T_1^*)}.
$$

Combining this with the relative-amplitude identity gives the post-peak balance

$$
-J_\alpha'(T_1^*)J_\alpha(T_1^*)
=
\frac{2}{T_1^*h(z_1^*)}
\int_0^{z_1^*}|\Delta Q|W_1^2h\,dz,
$$

conditional on positivity and zero-free hypotheses. This is a useful lemma, not a proof of a small-$\alpha$ theorem.

4. A3's endpoint ODE, cap length, quadratic $K_B$, and cap monotonicity are correct. In particular,

$$
K_B'(u_\sigma)
=
\frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}+\frac{\beta(n+1)}{2B},
$$

so in the residual strip $\alpha>1/2$ one has $K_B'(u)>1/4$ on the cap. This remains a residual-strip fact, not a global statement for all $\alpha,\beta\ge0$.

A theorem must split into: no zero of $K_B$ in the cap; first zero $u_0$ exists; $u_0=u_\sigma$; first critical point $u_1$ absent. This correction is important and should be retained in the formal endpoint-cap theorem.

6. The half-order Bessel maximum target is correct in form. For

$$
J_{1/2}(t)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi t}}\sin t,
$$

the first positive stationary point satisfies

$$
\tan t=2t,
$$

and the maximum is about $0.6791921047<0.680$. Extension from $\nu=1/2$ to all $\nu\ge1/2$ requires Landau's monotonicity theorem. The published abstract for Landau's paper states that $\sup_x|J_\nu(x)|$ strictly decreases from $1$ to $0$ as $\nu$ increases from $0$ to $\infty$, and also describes monotonicity at positive stationary points.
