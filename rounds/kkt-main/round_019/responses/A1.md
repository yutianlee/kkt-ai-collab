### 1. Previously certified endpoint reduction

I keep the Round 18 endpoint-cap structure as the base state. In the residual right endpoint range, the cap satisfies

$$
0\le u\le u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n.
$$

The endpoint equation is

$$
(p_BH')'+q_BH=0,
$$

and the Sonin product is

$$
K_B=p_Bq_B.
$$

The product is a concave quadratic,

$$
K_B(u)
=
-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+\Lambda_Bu-\Delta_Bu^2,
$$

and on the residual cap

$$
K_B'(u)\ge K_B'(u_\sigma)
=
\frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}+\frac{\beta(n+1)}{2B}.
$$

Thus for $\alpha>1/2$,

$$
K_B'(u)>\frac14.
$$

The endpoint solution is positive and increasing before the first zero of $K_B$, and the Sonin functional orders local extrema after the turning point. Therefore a cap failure can only occur at the first critical point $u_1$ after the first zero $u_0$ of $K_B$, if such a point exists. If no $u_1$ exists, the cap is controlled by monotonicity and the imported central boundary estimate.

This part remains conditional on the imported central, energy, small-exponent, and symmetry modules. It is not re-proved here.

### 2. Exact dynamic oscillator and Langer transformation

The dynamic variable

$$
\tau=\log\frac{u}{B-u}
$$

satisfies

$$
\frac{du}{d\tau}=p_B(u).
$$

Therefore

$$
H_\tau=p_BH',
$$

and from

$$
(p_BH')'+q_BH=0
$$

one obtains

$$
H_{\tau\tau}
=
p_B(p_BH')'
=
-p_Bq_BH
=
-K_BH.
$$

Thus

$$
H_{\tau\tau}+K(\tau)H=0,
\qquad
K(\tau)=K_B(u(\tau)).
$$

Define the Langer coordinate $\zeta$ by

$$
K(\tau)=\zeta(\tau)\zeta_\tau(\tau)^2.
$$

Then put

$$
H(\tau)=\zeta_\tau^{-1/2}W(\zeta).
$$

A standard differentiation gives

$$
W_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta W=\Psi_B(\zeta)W,
$$

with

$$
\Psi_B(\zeta)
=
\frac{\zeta K_{\tau\tau}}{4K^2}
-
\frac{5\zeta K_\tau^2}{16K^3}
+
\frac{5}{16\zeta^2}.

The apparent singularity at $\zeta=0$ is removable, with accepted Round 18 value

$$

This is the right residual object for the current route. It is the object A2 must stress-test on the $\theta=0$ Laguerre face, and A4 must measure.

At the first critical point of $H$,

$$
H_\tau(\tau_1)=0.
$$

Since

$$
W=\zeta_\tau^{1/2}H,

we have

$$
W_\zeta
=
\zeta_\tau^{-1/2}H_\tau

\frac12\zeta_{\tau\tau}\zeta_\tau^{-3/2}H.

At $\tau=\tau_1$ this becomes

$$
W_\zeta(\zeta_1)
=
\frac12\zeta_{\tau\tau}(\tau_1)\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^{-3/2}H(\tau_1).

Because

$$
H(\tau_1)=\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^{-1/2}W(\zeta_1),

we get the scalar logarithmic derivative condition

$$
\boxed{
W_\zeta(\zeta_1)=d_1W(\zeta_1),
\qquad
d_1=\frac{\zeta_{\tau\tau}(\tau_1)}{2\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^2}.

$$

Now write

$$
W(\zeta_1)=A_1a(\zeta_1)+B_1b(\zeta_1),
$$

and

$$
W_\zeta(\zeta_1)=A_1a'(\zeta_1)+B_1b'(\zeta_1).

The critical condition implies

$$
A_1\left(a'(\zeta_1)-d_1a(\zeta_1)\right)
+
B_1\left(b'(\zeta_1)-d_1b(\zeta_1)\right)
=
0.

Thus $(A_1,B_1)$ lies in the one-dimensional nullspace spanned by

$$
\left(
b'(\zeta_1)-d_1b(\zeta_1),

\right).

Let

$$
D_1=
\max\left(
|a'(\zeta_1)-d_1a(\zeta_1)|,\,
|b'(\zeta_1)-d_1b(\zeta_1)|
\right).
$$

Since

$$
a(\zeta)b'(\zeta)-a'(\zeta)b(\zeta)=-\frac1\pi,
$$

we obtain

$$
|W(\zeta_1)|
\le
\frac{\|Z(\zeta_1)\|_\infty}{\pi D_1}.
$$

Combining this with the coefficient variation bound gives

$$
|H(u_1)|
\le
\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^{-1/2}
\frac{
e^{\mathcal V_A}
\|Z(\zeta_{\mathrm{cut}})\|_\infty
}{
\pi D_1

}{
\pi D_1

T_{n,\alpha,\beta}.

This is the main Round 19 theorem-level refinement.

### 5. Denominator risk and falsification test

$$

contradicting the Airy Wronskian.

$$
D_1
\ge
\frac{1}{\pi(|a(\zeta_1)|+|b(\zeta_1)|)}.

This follows from

$$
|a(b'-d_1b)-b(a'-d_1a)|

\frac1\pi.

But this lower bound merely recovers a version of the crude envelope. The actual measured value of $D_1$ is decisive. A4 should report the ratio

### 7. Crude matrix theorem versus weighted Olver/Dunster--Gil--Segura theorem
