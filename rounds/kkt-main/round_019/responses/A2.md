### 1. Exact Limiting Topology of the Laguerre Face

We evaluate the structural behavior of the Langer residual at the $\theta=0$ Laguerre boundary face, corresponding to the limit $B \to \infty$.
The dynamic coordinate is $\tau = \log u$. The polynomial geometry simplifies to $p_\infty(u) = u$, and the invariant frequency product converges to:

$$K_\infty(u) = -\frac{\alpha^2}{4} + \Lambda_\infty u - \frac{u^2}{4}$$

where $\Lambda_\infty = n + \frac{\alpha+1}{2}$.
The first turning point $u_0$ satisfies $K_\infty(u_0) = 0$. For large $n$ and fixed parameters, expanding the quadratic root yields:

$$u_0 = 2\Lambda_\infty - 2\sqrt{\Lambda_\infty^2 - \alpha^2/4} \approx \frac{\alpha^2}{4\Lambda_\infty} \approx \frac{\alpha^2}{4n}$$

In the dynamic $\tau$ coordinate, $K(\tau) = K_\infty(e^\tau)$. The derivatives with respect to $\tau$ evaluate as:

$$K_\tau(\tau) = u K_u(u) = \Lambda_\infty u - \frac{u^2}{2}$$

$$K_{\tau\tau}(\tau) = u \frac{d}{du} K_\tau(u) = \Lambda_\infty u - u^2$$

$$K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau) = u \frac{d}{du} K_{\tau\tau}(u) = \Lambda_\infty u - 2u^2$$

Evaluating these terms strictly at the turning point $\tau_0 = \log u_0$:
Since $K_\infty(u_0) = 0$, we have $\Lambda_\infty u_0 - u_0^2/4 = \alpha^2/4$.
Substituting this relation into the derivatives gives:

$$\gamma = K_\tau(\tau_0) = \frac{\alpha^2}{4} - \frac{u_0^2}{4}$$

$$K_{\tau\tau}(\tau_0) = \frac{\alpha^2}{4} - \frac{3u_0^2}{4}$$

$$K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau_0) = \frac{\alpha^2}{4} - \frac{7u_0^2}{4}$$

### 2. Removable Singularity and Variation Scaling

The exact removable singularity of the Langer residual at $\zeta=0$ is given by:

$$\Psi_\infty(0) = \frac{10\gamma K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau_0) - 9 K_{\tau\tau}(\tau_0)^2}{140\gamma^{8/3}}$$

Inserting the derived exact evaluations for the numerator:

$$10 \gamma K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau_0) - 9 K_{\tau\tau}(\tau_0)^2 = 10 \left( \frac{\alpha^2}{4} - \frac{u_0^2}{4} \right) \left( \frac{\alpha^2}{4} - \frac{7u_0^2}{4} \right) - 9 \left( \frac{\alpha^2}{4} - \frac{3u_0^2}{4} \right)^2$$

Expanding and collecting terms:

$$= 10 \left( \frac{\alpha^4}{16} - \frac{8\alpha^2 u_0^2}{16} + \frac{7u_0^4}{16} \right) - 9 \left( \frac{\alpha^4}{16} - \frac{6\alpha^2 u_0^2}{16} + \frac{9u_0^4}{16} \right)$$

$$= \frac{\alpha^4}{16} - \frac{26\alpha^2 u_0^2}{16} - \frac{11u_0^4}{16}$$

In the asymptotic limit where $n \to \infty$ and $u_0 \to 0$, the numerator approaches precisely $\alpha^4/16$. The denominator approaches $140(\alpha^2/4)^{8/3}$. Thus:

$$\Psi_\infty(0) \approx \frac{\alpha^4/16}{140(\alpha^2/4)^{8/3}} = \frac{1}{140} \left( \frac{\alpha^2}{4} \right)^{-2/3} = \frac{4^{2/3}}{140} \alpha^{-4/3}$$

This derivation yields a useful structural diagnostic: for fixed parameters, $\Psi_\infty(0)$ is an order-one quantity independent of $n$ at the level of this local turning-point model. Because the mapping coordinate $\zeta_1$ is expected to scale to an order-one value representing the first wave peak, the total variation integral $\mathcal V_A \approx \Psi_\infty(0) \zeta_1$ should be treated as a possible order-one constant. This supports treating the variation barrier as real until weighted or interval measurement refines the constants.

### 4. Prüfer Phase Equation Buffer Tension

If one attempts a piecewise handoff to bypass the variation integral, the exact modified Prüfer phase equation dictates the dynamics:

$$\phi_\tau = \sqrt{K_B} + \frac{K_{B,\tau}}{4K_B}\sin 2\phi$$

Stable forward integration demands a strictly positive phase derivative. The unperturbed frequency must dominate the drift term:

$$\sqrt{K_B} > \left| \frac{K_{B,\tau}}{4K_B} \right| \implies K_B^{3/2} > \frac{K_{B,\tau}}{4}$$

Utilizing the local linear model $K_B(\tau) \approx \gamma(\tau-\tau_0)$ for $\tau > \tau_0$:

$$\gamma^{3/2} (\tau-\tau_0)^{3/2} > \frac{\gamma}{4} \implies \tau-\tau_0 > 4^{-2/3} \gamma^{-1/3}$$

$$u - u_0 > p_B(u_0) 4^{-2/3} \gamma^{-1/3}$$

Because $p_B(u_0) \approx u_0 \approx \alpha^2/4n$ and $\gamma \approx \alpha^2/4$:

$$u - u_0 > \frac{\alpha^2}{4n} 4^{-2/3} \left(\frac{\alpha^2}{4}\right)^{-1/3} = \frac{\alpha^{4/3}}{4^{4/3} n}$$

For parameters scaling as $\alpha = cn$, the required physical buffer is:

$$u - u_0 > \frac{c^{4/3} n^{4/3}}{4^{4/3} n} = \mathcal{O}(n^{1/3})$$

This macroscopic $\mathcal{O}(n^{1/3})$ distance explicitly quantifies the mandatory geometric buffer. Initiating a handoff too close to the turning point generates a singular boundary term, while initiating it at this buffer distance wildly violates the strict monotonicity and accuracy of the linear Airy approximation.
