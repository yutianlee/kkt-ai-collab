## 2. Claims that look correct

1. **Theta parametrisation for \(n=2\)** -- With \(B = n+\alpha+\beta+1 = \alpha+\beta+3\) and \(\theta = (\alpha+3)/B\), the inversion \(\beta = (\alpha+3)(1-\theta)/\theta\) and the range \(0\le\theta\le 1\) are algebraically exact. The cases \(\theta=0\) (Laguerre face) and \(\theta=1\) (\(\beta=0\) face) are handled by continuity.

2. **Cap endpoint \(u_\sigma\)** -- For \(n=2\), \(u_\sigma = \frac{2B}{B+1}\) matches the formula \(u_\sigma = nB/(B+n-1)\) given in A1's summary. This is consistent with standard Jacobi-to-endpoint maps (though the explicit map \(x\mapsto u\) is missing).

3. **Polynomial \(P_{2,\alpha,\theta}(u)\)** -- Assuming the Jacobi polynomial is normalized by \(P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(1)=\binom{n+\alpha}{n}\), the expression
   \[
   P_{2,\alpha,\theta}(u) = \frac{(\alpha+1)(\alpha+2)}2 - (\alpha+2)u + \frac12\Bigl(1+\frac{\theta}{\alpha+3}\Bigr)u^2
   \]
   follows from \(P_2^{(\alpha,\beta)}\bigl(1-\frac{2u}{B}\bigr)\) after substituting \(1/B = \theta/(\alpha+3)\). The constant term is the correct binomial coefficient. The verification will be trivial once the definition of \(H(u)\) (i.e., the weight inclusion) is pinned down.

4. **Lemma R28.4 (crude Gamma bound)** -- \(\Gamma(B)/\Gamma(B-\alpha) < B^\alpha\) for \(\alpha>0\) and \(B>\alpha\). This follows from \(\psi(t)<\log t\) and integration; it is a standard inequality. The condition \(B-\alpha = \beta+3>0\) is satisfied. This bound is safe for initial estimates.

5. **A2's symbolic cancellation** -- The computation
   \[
   h'(z_1^*) = -h(z_1^*)\frac{2\Lambda_B}{T_1^*}\frac{J_\alpha'(T_1^*)}{J_\alpha(T_1^*)}
   \]
   is exact, provided \(Y(z)=z^{1/2}H(u(z))\) and \(H'(u)=0\) imply \(Y'(z_1^*) = \frac{1}{2z_1^*}Y(z_1^*)\). The resulting identity for \(F(T_1^*) = -J_\alpha'(T_1^*)J_\alpha(T_1^*)\) is algebraically sound. The Volterra step \((W_1^2 h')' = -\Delta Q\,W_1\,Y\) is standard and, with the explicit \(\Delta Q\) given by A2, yields the stated integral relation.

6. **Whittaker reference dismissed** -- A2's heuristic argument that the Whittaker equation is not needed is plausible; the Bessel reference already captures the dominant \(1/z\) and \(1/z^2\) terms. This decision avoids unnecessary complexity and is reasonable.

## 3. Claims that need proof

1. **The cubic \(C_{\alpha,\theta}(u)\)** -- A1 says: "The exact first-critical-point equation, after multiplying by the positive denominator \(2\theta(\alpha+3)\) ... is the cubic \(C_{\alpha,\theta}(u)=0\)".
   - The derivation from the definition of \(H(u)\) to this cubic is **not shown**. \(H(u)\) normally includes a weight factor \((u)^{\alpha/2}(1-u/B)^{\beta/2}\) (or similar) times the Jacobi polynomial. Setting its derivative to zero yields a rational equation; clearing denominators and simplifying should produce a cubic. Without the intermediate steps, the coefficients cannot be trusted.
   - The displayed cubic contains large integer combinations (e.g., \(3\alpha^2-\alpha\theta+17\alpha+24\)). A single sign mistake or missing factor of \(B\) would invalidate it.
   - The cubic is claimed to be the **first** critical point; it is necessary to prove that the relevant root lies in \((0,u_\sigma)\) and is the smallest positive root where the derivative vanishes. A rigorous root-isolation argument (Sturm sequence or interval Newton) must accompany the cubic.

2. **Lemma R28.3 -- the degree-two target ratio** -- The expression for \(\frac{H_2(u)^4}{T_{2,\alpha,\theta}^4}\) contains factors
   \[
   \Bigl(\frac{2\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(\alpha+3)\Gamma(B-\alpha)}\Bigr)^2
   \Bigl(\frac{u}{B}\Bigr)^{2\alpha}
   \Bigl(1-\frac{u}{B}\Bigr)^{2(B-\alpha-3)}
   P_{2,\alpha,\theta}(u)^4
   \frac{\alpha+3-\alpha\theta}{3}.
   \]
   The term \(T_{2,\alpha,\theta}^4\) was defined earlier as \(3/(\alpha+3-\alpha\theta)\); the combination is consistent with the claim that the whole ratio must be \(<1\). However, the factor \(2\) in the Gamma combination and the exponent \(2(B-\alpha-3)\) are sensitive to the exact normalization of \(H\) and the map \(x\mapsto u\). A complete derivation starting from the Jacobi orthonormal functions (or the KKT discrete Laguerre normalization) is required.

3. **Endpoint-cap ODE reduction** -- Both A1's "certified endpoint-cap spine" and A2's rational-Bessel normal form depend on the existence and explicit form of the transformed equation
   \[
   (p_B(u)H'(u))' + q_B(u)H(u) = 0,
   \]
   together with the expressions
   \[
   p_B(u) = u\Bigl(1-\frac{u}{B}\Bigr),\qquad
   q_B(u) = n+\frac12-\frac{n+1}{2B} - \frac{\bigl((1-\frac{n+1}{B})u-\alpha\bigr)^2}{4u(1-\frac{u}{B})},
   \]
   and the definitions of \(\Lambda_B,\Delta_B,K_B\). These formulas are **not re-derived** in the present outputs. They must be provided with the transformation \(x\mapsto u\) and a verification that the Jacobi ODE exactly converts to this form. Until this is done, all subsequent claims about \(K_B\), cap monotonicity, and Sonin functionals are unsubstantiated.

4. **Rational-Bessel residual \(\Delta Q(z)\)** -- A2 states
   \[
   \Delta Q(z) = -\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z} - \frac{\Delta_B B^2}{(B+z)^2},
   \]
   and claims it is strictly negative and bounded by \(M_Q\). The derivation of this expression from the \((p_B,q_B)\) form and the change of variable \(z = Bu/(B-u)\) is not shown. It must be verified algebraically and the bound \(M_Q \le 2.75\) (uniformly in the residual strip) must be proved.

5. **Zero-safety bootstrap theorem** -- A2 proposes that if
   \[
   \frac{M_Q (j_{\alpha,1})^3}{16\Lambda_B^2} J_\alpha(j'_{\alpha,1})^2 < F_{\max}
   \]
   then \(T_1^* < j_{\alpha,1}\). This is a plausible "topological" condition, but it depends on:
   - the monotonic increase of \(F(T) = -J_\alpha'(T)J_\alpha(T)\) on \([j'_{\alpha,1}, j_{\alpha,1}]\),
   - explicit lower bounds for \(F_{\max}\),
   - explicit bounds for \(j_{\alpha,1}\) and \(j_{\alpha,1}-j'_{\alpha,1}\),
   - a rigorous Gamma envelope for the normalization constant that appears in the bound for \(h(z)\) and thus in the integral estimate.
   None of these numerical ingredients are supplied; the constants \(c_1,c_2,c_3\) and their sources in Watson (1944) are merely mentioned. The theorem therefore remains **conditional**.

6. **Finite certificate criterion (Lemma R28.6)** -- The proposed box certificate uses interval arithmetic on the log-ratio and the cubic. The logical chain from "\(K_B\) has no cap zero and \(C_{\alpha,\theta}\) has a unique root in \(U_1\)" to "the cap estimate is certified" relies on the endpoint-cap first-lobe theorem itself. That theorem is not yet fully proved or even stated with its hypotheses. The criterion must be turned into a theorem with a formal proof that reduces the check to the listed conditions.

## 4. Possible errors or hidden assumptions

- **Hidden assumption: correctness of the global endpoint-cap reduction** -- The entire first-lobe argument (forbidden-zone ascent, Sonin functional, first-critical-point inequality) is imported from earlier rounds but **not re-examined**. If the transformation \(x\mapsto u\) contains an error, or if \(p_B,q_B\) do not precisely match the stated forms, the foundation collapses. This is the single largest risk in the current proof strategy.

- **Algebraic slip in the cubic coefficients** -- The cubic is presented without any intermediate simplification. A mis-scaled factor (e.g., using \(B\) instead of \(B+1\) early on) could have propagated. A1 acknowledges earlier \(B\) vs \(B+1\) errors. The current cubic is said to be "newly derived and verified algebraically in this reasoning," but the verification is not shown. I would assign a moderate probability of an undetected slip.

- **Interpretation of the Laguerre face \(\theta=0\)** -- The cubic was obtained by multiplying by \(2\theta(\alpha+3)\) and then extending to \(\theta=0\). This procedure could introduce a spurious root at \(\theta=0\) if the original equation had a denominator that vanished. The continuous extension must be justified by checking that the limit \(\theta\to0\) of the critical point equation correctly reproduces the direct derivative of the Laguerre limit. This needs a separate check.

- **Mis-identification of the first critical point** -- The cubic may have three real roots; the smallest positive root may not be the first critical point if the derivative changes sign between them or if the cubic root does not correspond to a valid \(u\) (due to domain restrictions). The box certificate requires that the chosen root is the unique one in \((0,u_\sigma)\); a Sturm-sequence argument or an interval Newton with existence proof is mandatory.

- **A2's Volterra integral from \(0\) to \(z_1^*\)** -- The reference solution \(W_1(z) = \sqrt{z}J_\alpha(2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z})\) is the regular solution at \(z=0\). The relative amplitude \(h(z)\) is defined as \(Y/W_1\). For the integration to be valid, \(W_1\) must not vanish on \((0,z_1^*]\). This is exactly the zero-safety condition being proved; but the identity itself holds as long as \(h\) is well-defined. However, the bound on the integral that A2 later uses (the pre-peak Volterra bound) assumes \(h\) is bounded by an exponential, which is fine near \(0\). The risk is that the bound's constant depends on the normalisation and may blow up if \(W_1\) becomes very small, but the exponential bound does not rely on that.

- **Monotonicity of \(F(T) = -J_\alpha'(T)J_\alpha(T)\)** -- This function is not universally increasing; its derivative changes sign. On \([j'_{\alpha,1}, j_{\alpha,1}]\) it is indeed positive and rises from \(0\) to some maximum and then decreases to \(0\)? Actually, at \(j_{\alpha,1}\), \(J_\alpha=0\), so \(F=0\). At \(j'_{\alpha,1}\), \(J_\alpha'=0\), so \(F=0\). It increases then decreases. So the monotonicity claim from peak to zero is false: it is not monotonic increasing; it has a single maximum. The bootstrap argument likely uses the maximum value \(F_{\max}\) and the fact that \(F(T_1^*)< F_{\max}\) for \(T_1^*< j_{\alpha,1}\). That is trivially true. The condition is that the bound on \(F(T_1^*)\) is less than \(F_{\max}\). So the topological condition is plausible. A2 says "On the interval \([j'_{\alpha,1}, j_{\alpha,1}]\), \(F(T)\) increases from \(0\) to a maximum \(F_{\max}\)". That is incorrect; it increases then decreases. This is a small error: they mean it increases to the maximum and then decreases, but the maximum is achieved at some point. So the statement "\(F(T)\) increases" is not exactly correct but does not invalidate the idea if they use the maximum value. I flag it as a minor inaccuracy.

- **Uniform bound \(M_Q\le 2.75\)** -- This number is given without derivation and may depend on the parameter range. It is plausible if \(\Lambda_B\) and \(\Delta_B\) are bounded, but a verification is required.

- **Gamma-ratio constant in the target ratio** -- The factor \(2\) in \(\frac{2\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(\alpha+3)\Gamma(B-\alpha)}\) might be off by a factor related to the Jacobi polynomial normalization. In the KKT paper, the discrete Laguerre operator eigenfunctions are normalized differently; a missing factor could shift the inequality by a power of \(2\) or \(\sqrt{\pi}\). The safe approach is to write the ratio directly in terms of the standard Jacobi weight and the known norm, avoiding ad-hoc constants.

The interval arithmetic can be implemented in a proof-assistant--like manner, but the certificate theorem (Lemma R28.6) must be proved as a meta-theorem that reduces the inequality to checked conditions.

- **A2's rational-Bessel module for small \(\alpha\)** -- The phase-shift identity is correct and can be used to derive an **analytic** bound for \(\alpha\) up to an explicit multiple of \(n^{3/5}\). This would cover a region where finite subdivision is thin (near \(\alpha=1/2\)) and give a direct proof without boxes. The required Bessel constants must be extracted from Watson and combined with a rigorous Gamma envelope (Binet/Wendel bounds). This module should be completed as a separate lemma.

- **Langer/Airy for the bulk** -- For the regime \(\alpha\) large (e.g., \(\alpha\ge C n^{3/5}\)), neither the finite certificate nor the Bessel comparison is efficient. The Langer/Airy track (uniform asymptotic expansion with explicit remainder) must be instantiated with error bounds from Olver or Dunster--Gil--Segura. This track is not addressed in the current outputs; it remains a necessary piece.

- **Avoid over-reliance on any single track** -- The multi-pronged architecture is sound, but each piece must be certified independently before integration. No synthesis step should assume the conclusion of another.
