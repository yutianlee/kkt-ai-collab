## Claims that need proof

### A1

1.  **Removable value of $\Psi_B(0)$** – A1 derives it by a Taylor expansion of the Langer map, but a formal CAS cancellation log is still absent. The expression involves high‑order cancellations; a transcription error would poison all subsequent variation estimates. This must be certified by A3 or an explicit machine‑readable expansion.  
2.  **Cut‑off coefficient $\mathfrak C_c$** – The coefficient is exactly computable from $H(u_c)$ and $H_\tau(u_c)$, but A1 gives no bound on it over the residual domain. The theorem remains conditional until $\mathfrak C_c$ is controlled, either by a DGS/Olver initialization theorem or by interval computation at the cut.  
3.  **Convergence of the Airy envelope** – The bound uses $\mathfrak m(\xi)^{2}=\operatorname{Ai}(-\xi)^{2}+\operatorname{Bi}(-\xi)^{2}$, which decays like $1/\sqrt{\xi}$ for large $\xi$. The integrability of $\mathfrak m(\xi)^{2}|\Psi_B(\xi)|$ on $[\zeta_c,\infty)$ is plausible but not proved; this particularly needs verification near $\zeta_1$ where $K_B$ is small again.  
4.  **The conditional inequality** – The entire result is a conditional statement; none of the numeric constants have been instantiated, so the claim “the route is now measurable” is true in principle but does not reduce the proof gap.  

### A2

1.  **Rational‑Bessel Volterra scaling** – The bound $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{Bess}}=\mathcal O(\alpha^{3}/n^{2})$ relies on the Nicholson envelope $|J_{\alpha}Y_{\alpha}|\le (\pi\sqrt{\alpha^{2}-x^{2}})^{-1}$, which is only valid for $x<\alpha$. The integration in the pre‑peak region is correct, but the oscillatory tail $[\alpha,x_1]$ is **not controlled** by the same bound. A2 acknowledges this, but the claimed asymptotic scaling cannot be promoted to a proof until the tail contribution is explicitly bounded.  
2.  **Occurrence of $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{Bess}}$ in final margin** – Even if the variation scales favourably, no complete inequality connecting $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{Bess}}$, the normalization $M_{n,\alpha,B}$, and the KKT target is stated. The gap between scaling heuristics and a closed conditional theorem is large.  
3.  **Overlap condition** – The threshold $\alpha=C\sqrt n$ is conjectured from scaling arguments; no rigorous threshold is derived. The transition between Bessel‑dominant and Langer‑dominant regimes must be quantified before a split‑proof can be assembled.  
4.  **Gamma‑ratio Binet bound** – The Binet formula is correctly recalled, but the explicit numerical constants for the four‑argument combination $\frac12[\log\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)+\log\Gamma(B)-\log\Gamma(n+1)-\log\Gamma(B-\alpha)]$ are not carried through to a usable upper bound. The remainder estimate $\pm\frac{1}{24w}$ is too weak to cover $\alpha$ as large as $n$ in the transition strip; the Binet remainder would be $\mathcal O(1/\alpha)$, which is not $o(1)$ when $\alpha=cn$. This point must be upgraded before the gamma‑ratio envelope can be called a lemma.  

### A4

1.  **Analytic degree‑one certificate** – The bound
    $$
    H_1(u)^{2}\le \frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha+2)}u^{\alpha}(\alpha+1-u)^{2}
    $$
    uses the Wendel‑Gautschi inequality $\Gamma(B)/\Gamma(B-\alpha)\le B^{\alpha}$ for $\alpha\le1$. A4 extends it to $\alpha\in(1,1.2]$ by a plausible argument, but this extension is not proved with a named theorem. The one‑variable maximum $\max_{[1/2,1.2]} V(\alpha)/\Gamma(\alpha+2)^{2}$ is computed heuristically; no certified interval or analytic monotonicity is supplied. Therefore the claimed margin $0.235$ is not yet certified. The statement should be downgraded to “plausible analytic bound requiring two checkable inequalities,” not “derived under assumptions” without qualification.  
2.  **Gamma‑ratio heuristic** – The regime‑split envelope ($M\le1+C/(n+1)$ for $\alpha\le C\sqrt n$, $M\le e^{-cn}$ for $\alpha=cn$) is a useful guide, but A4’s Binet‑remainder estimates are not carried out with explicit signed terms. The claim $\log M\le -c n$ for $\alpha=cn$ assumes that the entropy negativity $f(c)<0$ dominates the $\mathcal O(\log n)$ terms from the Binet formula; a complete inequality must display the $\log n$ terms and prove they are absorbed.

Before an interval certificate can be executed, the cubic must be independently verified against direct differentiation of the compactified polynomial. A3 is the appropriate auditor for this.  

## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

### A1

- The Airy‑envelope propagation assumes $W$ is the recessive solution; the Volterra kernel is symmetric, so no recessive assumption is needed, but the norm $\mathfrak C_c$ is computed from Cauchy data at $\zeta_c$ that may contain both $\operatorname{Ai}$ and $\operatorname{Bi}$ components. The bound does not exploit the recessive nature of the true solution, which may cause overestimation.  
- The formula for $\Psi_B(0)$ given by A1 may contain a transcription error: the coefficient $140$ in the denominator should be double‑checked against the standard Schwarzian expansion. A3’s earlier Round 20 value was $140\gamma^{8/3}$; A1 uses the same, but a sign error in the numerator is possible.  
- A1 states that the Airy Wronskian is $1/\pi$, which is correct in the conventions $\operatorname{Ai}(x)\operatorname{Bi}'(x)-\operatorname{Ai}'(x)\operatorname{Bi}(x)=1/\pi$. However, in some literature the Wronskian is $1/\pi$; it is important to verify the sign and the factor of $\pi$ in the Volterra kernel construction. A1’s factor of $\pi$ in the exponential bound appears consistent.  

### A2

- The Wronskian for the defined $W_1,W_2$ is derived as $1/\pi$, but this must be checked with the derivative of $\sqrt{z} J_{\alpha}(2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z})$ and $\sqrt{z} Y_{\alpha}(2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z})$. The factor of $2\Lambda_B$ in the chain rule could affect the prefactor; A2’s derivation should be audited by A3. Even if the constant is off by a factor, the qualitative scaling remains.  
- The “interlacing property” for $Y_{\alpha}$ and $J_{\alpha}$ at $\alpha\ge1/2$: it is true that the first positive zero of $Y_{\alpha}$ is greater than the first positive zero of $J_{\alpha}$? Actually, for $\nu>-1$, the zeros of $Y_{\nu}$ and $J_{\nu}$ interlace; but the claim that $Y_{\alpha}$ is negative up to the first critical point of $H$ may be false if $H$’s first critical point lies beyond the first zero of $Y_{\alpha}$. This needs verification.  
- The Nicholson bound $\pi |J_{\alpha}Y_{\alpha}|\le 1/\sqrt{\alpha^{2}-x^{2}}$ is for $x<\alpha$ and for real orders $\alpha\ge0$; but it originates from an asymptotic expansion; its rigorous validity in the form with monotonic integral representations is known (Watson’s treatise §13.6). However, the constant $\pi$ might not be exactly that; the bound $|J_{\nu}(x)Y_{\nu}(x)|\le \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi^{2}x^{2}-\nu^{2}}}$? That’s different. A2 should check the exact reference.  

### A4

- The degree‑one envelope uses $\frac{\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(B-\alpha)}\le B^{\alpha}$, which is valid for $\alpha\in[0,1]$ by the log‑convexity of the gamma function and Wendel’s inequality; for $\alpha>1$ the inequality can fail. A4’s handling of the $1<\alpha\le1.2$ case via $B-1-\delta$ is algebraically messy and not backed by a standard theorem. Even if the function $(\alpha+1-u)^{2}$ provides sufficient room, the Gamma‑ratio inequality must be proved or replaced by a known result such as Kershaw or Gautschi for the whole interval.  
- A4 labels several items as [PROVED] that are in fact heuristic or derived under assumptions. For example, the “Langer Residual Cancellation Audit” is labeled [PROVED] but only states that the Taylor expansion confirms cancellation; no CAS log or explicit terms are shown. This is misleading.  
- The $n=2$ cubic coefficient $C_2$ is given as $c_1(\alpha B+4B)+b_1(\alpha+\beta+2)$. The derivation from the critical equation $(\alpha(B-u)-\beta u)P_2(u)+2u(B-u)P_2'(u)=0$ must be recalculated; the term $c_1(\alpha B+4B)$ seems incomplete because it should involve the product of $c_1$ with contributions from both $\alpha(B-u)P_2$ and $2u(B-u)P_2'$. This needs A3’s independent check.  

## Suggested synthesis

The proof architecture should be frozen as a **regime‑split endpoint‑cap first‑lobe route** with three sub‑problems:

1. **Low‑degree certification** ($n=1,2$) – A4’s degree‑one analytic envelope (once the two needed inequalities are rigorously checked) and an interval certificate for $n=2$ using the corrected cubic. This can be completed independently of the asymptotic tracks.

2. **Bulk weighted Langer track** ($\alpha$ mesoscopic and above) – A1’s allowed‑side Airy‑envelope propagation is a suitable baseline. To become a proof, it needs:
   - a certified value of $\Psi_B(0)$ from A3’s CAS verification;
   - an upper bound for $\mathfrak C_c$ via a Frobenius‑to‑Airy connection (either a DGS/Olver theorem or a validated interval computation at the cut);
   - a measurable estimate of the variation integral $\mathcal V_A$ on a representative hard box.
   The sharpness can be improved by replacing the crude $\mathfrak m^{2}$ weight with a DGS weight once the external theorem is instantiated.

3. **Small‑α hard‑edge track** – The rational‑coordinate Bessel model (A2) is algebraically clean. The next step is not to claim $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{Bess}}=\mathcal O(\alpha^{3}/n^{2})$ as a proof, but to turn it into a **concrete Volterra inequality with explicit kernel and remainder**. Specifically:
   - bound the oscillatory tail $[\alpha,x_1]$ using Airy‑type estimates or Weber‑Schafheitlin integrals;
   - derive a strict upper bound for $\mathcal V_{\mathrm{Bess}}$ in terms of $\alpha,n,B$ that can be checked by interval arithmetic;
   - determine the actual overlap threshold $\alpha_{\mathrm{thresh}}(n)$ where the Bessel and Langer tracks meet, even if numerically.

The semi‑discrete inductive route (contiguous relations) appears obstructed by sign‑irregularities; it should be shelved unless a new positivity identity is found.

All three tracks share a common dependency: a **certified Gamma‑ratio envelope** for $M_{n,\alpha,B}$ (and its Langer analogue). This envelope must be split into at least three regimes ($\alpha=O(1)$, $\alpha=O(\sqrt n)$, $\alpha=cn$) and proved using a named real‑variable gamma inequality (e.g., Kershaw, Gautschi, or a Binet formula with explicit remainder bounds). A3 should produce this lemma as a priority.
