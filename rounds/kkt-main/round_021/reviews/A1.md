$$
K_B(u)=p_B(u)q_B(u)

-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+\Lambda_Bu-\Delta_Bu^2

with

$$

\Delta_B=\frac{c_B}{B}+\frac{r_B^2}{4}

is correct.

3. **Cap length.** The cap boundary

$$
u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n
$$

for $n\ge1$ remains certified.

4. **Cap monotonicity.** A3’s formula

$$
K_B'(u_\sigma)
=
\frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}+\frac{\beta(n+1)}{2B}
$$

is correct. Hence, because $K_B$ is concave and $K_B'$ decreases in $u$,

$$
K_B'(u)\ge K_B'(u_\sigma)\ge\frac{\alpha}{2}
$$

on $0\le u\le u_\sigma$. In the residual right endpoint range $\alpha>1/2$, this gives the required $K_B'(u)>1/4$.

5. **Forbidden-zone and no-zero case.** The qualitative logic remains correct: since $K_B(0)=-\alpha^2/4<0$ for $\alpha>0$, if $K_B$ has no zero in the cap then $K_B<0$ throughout the cap. If a first zero $u_0$ exists, the Frobenius branch is positive and increasing before $u_0$, so there is no forbidden-zone local maximum. This depends on the already-accepted sign argument for $W=p_BH'$.

6. **Dynamic oscillator.** A3’s exact transformation

is correct.

=
\sqrt{K_B}

\frac{K_{B,\tau}}{4K_B}\sin2\phi.

These are algebraically correct but still not an amplitude theorem.
