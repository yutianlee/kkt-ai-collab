Detailed reasoning:

### Instantiated DGS/Olver theorem

I choose the Airy simple-turning-point framework in Olver/DLMF §2.8(iii), supplemented by the Dunster--Gil--Segura 2019/2020 error-bound program, as the external theorem family. DLMF §2.8(iii) is directly relevant because it is the “Case II: Simple Turning Point” Airy framework and explicitly introduces Airy envelope functions; Dunster--Gil--Segura give computable explicit error bounds for the same simple-turning-point setting.

However, the fully sharp DGS theorem is still not instantiated here because I do not have the exact DGS weight denominator and slowly varying coefficient bounds in the local packet. To avoid another placeholder, I instantiate a weaker but completely explicit Airy-envelope propagation theorem on the allowed side $\zeta>0$. This theorem is not asymptotic. It follows directly from variation of constants for the exact transformed equation.

#### Post-turning Airy-envelope propagation theorem

Let $0<\zeta_c<\zeta_1$, and let $W$ solve

$$
W''+\zeta W=\Psi(\zeta)W
$$

on $[\zeta_c,\zeta_1]$, with

$$
\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta_1}
\mathfrak m(\xi)^2|\Psi(\xi)|\,d\xi<\infty,
$$

where

$$
A(\zeta)=\operatorname{Ai}(-\zeta),\qquad
B_A(\zeta)=\operatorname{Bi}(-\zeta),
$$

and

$$
\mathfrak m(\zeta)=\left(A(\zeta)^2+B_A(\zeta)^2\right)^{1/2}.
$$

Let derivatives with respect to $\zeta$ be denoted by dots:

$$
\dot A(\zeta)=\frac{d}{d\zeta}\operatorname{Ai}(-\zeta)
=-\operatorname{Ai}'(-\zeta),
$$

$$
\dot B_A(\zeta)=\frac{d}{d\zeta}\operatorname{Bi}(-\zeta)
=-\operatorname{Bi}'(-\zeta).
$$

At $\zeta_c$, define the Airy coefficient vector $(a_c,b_c)$ by

$$
W(\zeta_c)=a_cA(\zeta_c)+b_cB_A(\zeta_c),
$$

$$
W'(\zeta_c)=a_c\dot A(\zeta_c)+b_c\dot B_A(\zeta_c).
$$

Since

$$
A(\zeta)\dot B_A(\zeta)-\dot A(\zeta)B_A(\zeta)=-\frac1\pi,
$$

the coefficients are

$$
a_c
=
\pi\left(B_A(\zeta_c)W'(\zeta_c)-\dot B_A(\zeta_c)W(\zeta_c)\right),
$$

and

$$
b_c
=
\pi\left(\dot A(\zeta_c)W(\zeta_c)-A(\zeta_c)W'(\zeta_c)\right).
$$

Set

$$
\mathfrak C_c=\left(a_c^2+b_c^2\right)^{1/2}.
$$

Then

$$
|W(\zeta_1)|
\le
\mathfrak m(\zeta_1)
\mathfrak C_c
\exp\left(
\pi\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta_1}
\mathfrak m(\xi)^2|\Psi(\xi)|\,d\xi
\right).
$$

Proof sketch. Variation of constants gives

$$
W(\zeta)
=
a_cA(\zeta)+b_cB_A(\zeta)
+
\pi\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta}
\left(
A(\zeta)B_A(s)-B_A(\zeta)A(s)
\right)
\Psi(s)W(s)\,ds.
$$

The kernel satisfies

$$
\left|
A(\zeta)B_A(s)-B_A(\zeta)A(s)
\right|
\le
\mathfrak m(\zeta)\mathfrak m(s).
$$

Thus, after dividing by $\mathfrak m(\zeta)$,

$$
\frac{|W(\zeta)|}{\mathfrak m(\zeta)}
\le
\mathfrak C_c
+
\pi\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta}
\mathfrak m(s)^2|\Psi(s)|
\frac{|W(s)|}{\mathfrak m(s)}\,ds.
$$

Gronwall gives the displayed estimate.

Status: proved as an elementary Airy-kernel lemma. It is weaker than a full DGS/Olver turning-point theorem because it starts at $\zeta_c>0$ and does not bound $\mathfrak C_c$ from endpoint Frobenius data.

### KKT hypothesis check

The KKT dynamic oscillator is

$$
H_{\tau\tau}+K_B(u(\tau))H=0,
$$

where

$$
\tau=\log\frac{u}{B-u},\qquad
u(\tau)=\frac{Be^\tau}{1+e^\tau},
$$

and

$$
K_B(u)
=
-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+\Lambda_Bu-\Delta_Bu^2.
$$

The cap is

$$
0\le u\le u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n.
$$

In the residual right-endpoint strip,

$$
n\ge1,\qquad
\frac12<\alpha<\alpha_E(n),\qquad
\beta\ge0,
$$

we have

$$
K_B(0)=-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}<0.
$$

If right overflow occurs, $K_B$ has a first zero $u_0\in(0,u_\sigma)$, and

$$
K_B'(u)\ge K_B'(u_\sigma)
=
\frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
\ge\frac{\alpha}{2}
>
\frac14
$$

throughout the cap. Thus

$$
K_B'(u_0)>0.
$$

Since

$$
\frac{du}{d\tau}=p_B(u)=u\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right),
$$

the derivative of $K(\tau)=K_B(u(\tau))$ at the turning point is

$$
\gamma:=K_\tau(\tau_0)=p_B(u_0)K_B'(u_0)>0.
$$

Therefore the turning point is simple in the $\tau$ variable. This checks the simple-turning-point hypothesis needed for an Airy/Langer transformation.

Define $\zeta$ by

$$
K(\tau)=\zeta(\tau)\zeta_\tau(\tau)^2,
$$

with

$$
\zeta(\tau_0)=0,\qquad
\operatorname{sgn}\zeta=\operatorname{sgn}K.
$$

Equivalently, for $\tau>\tau_0$,

$$
\frac23\zeta(\tau)^{3/2}
=
\int_{\tau_0}^{\tau}\sqrt{K(s)}\,ds,
$$

and for $\tau<\tau_0$,

$$
\frac23(-\zeta(\tau))^{3/2}
=
\int_{\tau}^{\tau_0}\sqrt{-K(s)}\,ds.
$$

Near the turning point,

$$
\zeta_\tau(\tau_0)=\gamma^{1/3}>0.
$$

Now set

$$
H(\tau)=\zeta_\tau(\tau)^{-1/2}W(\zeta).
$$

A direct Liouville calculation gives

$$
W_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta W=\Psi_B(\zeta)W,
$$

where

$$
\Psi_B(\zeta)
=
\frac{\zeta K_{\tau\tau}}{4K^2}
-
\frac{5\zeta K_\tau^2}{16K^3}
+
\frac{5}{16\zeta^2}.
$$

This formula is valid away from $\zeta=0$ and has a removable singularity at $\zeta=0$.

The removable value is as follows. If

$$
K(\tau)=\gamma t+\frac{k_2}{2}t^2+\frac{k_3}{6}t^3+O(t^4),
\qquad
t=\tau-\tau_0,
$$

where

$$
k_2=K_{\tau\tau}(\tau_0),\qquad
k_3=K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau_0),
$$

then

$$
\Psi_B(0)
=
\frac{
10\gamma k_3-9k_2^2
}{
140\gamma^{8/3}
}.
$$

Derivation. Write

$$
\zeta
=
\gamma^{1/3}t
\left(
1+\frac{k_2}{10\gamma}t+
\left(
\frac{k_3}{42\gamma}
-
\frac{2k_2^2}{175\gamma^2}
\right)t^2
+O(t^3)
\right).
$$

Substituting this expansion into

$$
\Psi_B(\zeta)
=
\frac{\zeta K_{\tau\tau}}{4K^2}
-
\frac{5\zeta K_\tau^2}{16K^3}
+
\frac{5}{16\zeta^2}
$$

cancels the $\zeta^{-2}$ and $\zeta^{-1}$ terms and yields the displayed constant.

Status: algebraically derived here, but I still assign A3 to produce the formal CAS log because this removable value is a high-risk expression for transcription errors.

### Weighted variation formula

I choose the allowed-side propagation interval

$$
[\zeta_c,\zeta_1]\subset(0,\infty),
$$

where:

- $\zeta_c>0$ is a fixed allowed-side cut;
- $u_c$ is defined by $\zeta(u_c)=\zeta_c$;
- $u_1$ is the first critical point of $H$ after $u_0$;
- $\zeta_1=\zeta(u_1)$.

The actual, non-placeholder weighted variation is

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal V_A(\zeta_c,\zeta_1)
=
\pi\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta_1}
\mathfrak m(\xi)^2|\Psi_B(\xi)|\,d\xi.
}
$$

Here

$$
\mathfrak m(\xi)^2
=
\operatorname{Ai}(-\xi)^2+\operatorname{Bi}(-\xi)^2.
$$

This is the Airy-envelope weight produced by the exact kernel bound

$$
|A(\zeta)B_A(s)-B_A(\zeta)A(s)|
\le
\mathfrak m(\zeta)\mathfrak m(s).
$$

This formula is deliberately weaker than a sharp DGS weight but has two advantages:

1. it is fully explicit;
2. it is directly implementable by A4 with interval arithmetic.

For large positive $\xi$, the Airy modulus has the standard oscillatory scale

$$
\mathfrak m(\xi)^2\asymp \frac{1}{\pi \sqrt{\xi}},
$$

so the variation resembles

$$
\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta_1}
\frac{|\Psi_B(\xi)|}{\sqrt{\xi}}\,d\xi
$$

up to absolute constants. This is the correct qualitative damping in the allowed region. No exponential $\operatorname{Bi}$ weight appears because the cut is chosen with $\zeta_c>0$.

At the cut, the exact transformed Cauchy data are:

$$
W_c=\zeta_\tau(\tau_c)^{1/2}H(\tau_c),
$$

and

$$
W'_c
=
\zeta_\tau(\tau_c)^{-1/2}H_\tau(\tau_c)
+
\frac12
\frac{\zeta_{\tau\tau}(\tau_c)}{\zeta_\tau(\tau_c)^{3/2}}
H(\tau_c).
$$

Then

$$
a_c
=
\pi\left(B_A(\zeta_c)W'_c-\dot B_A(\zeta_c)W_c\right),
$$

$$
b_c
=
\pi\left(\dot A(\zeta_c)W_c-A(\zeta_c)W'_c\right),
$$

and

$$
\boxed{
\mathfrak C_c=(a_c^2+b_c^2)^{1/2}.
}
$$

This constant is not a placeholder: it is exactly computable from $H(u_c)$ and $H_\tau(u_c)$, hence from the finite hypergeometric polynomial. The missing proof issue is that it is not yet bounded analytically over the whole residual domain.

### Conditional inequality

The strong KKT target at the first lobe is

$$
|H(u_1)|
\le
T_{n,\alpha,\beta},
$$

where

$$
T_{n,\alpha,\beta}
=
\left(
\frac{(n+1)B}{(n+\alpha+1)(B-\alpha)}
\right)^{1/4}.
$$

Because

$$
H(\tau)=\zeta_\tau(\tau)^{-1/2}W(\zeta),
$$

the allowed-side Airy-envelope bound gives

$$
|H(u_1)|
\le
\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^{-1/2}
\mathfrak m(\zeta_1)
\mathfrak C_c
\exp\left(
\mathcal V_A(\zeta_c,\zeta_1)
\right).
$$

Therefore the following is a theorem-level conditional estimate.

#### Conditional Langer first-lobe certificate

Fix $n\ge1$, $\frac12<\alpha<\alpha_E(n)$, $\beta\ge0$, and suppose the residual right endpoint cap has a first turning point $u_0$ and first critical point $u_1$. Let $\zeta_c>0$ be an allowed-side cut with $\zeta_c<\zeta_1$. If

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal R_{\mathrm{Lang}}
:=
\frac{
\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^{-1/2}
\mathfrak m(\zeta_1)
\mathfrak C_c
\exp\left(
\mathcal V_A(\zeta_c,\zeta_1)
\right)
}{
T_{n,\alpha,\beta}
}
\le1,
}
$$

then the KKT first-lobe estimate holds at $u_1$.

This conditional theorem is exact. It is not yet a global proof because $\mathcal R_{\mathrm{Lang}}\le1$ has not been established analytically or computationally.

A useful equivalent form eliminates $\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)$ using

$$
\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)=\sqrt{\frac{K(\tau_1)}{\zeta_1}},
$$

valid in the allowed region. Thus

$$
\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^{-1/2}
=
\left(\frac{\zeta_1}{K(\tau_1)}\right)^{1/4}.
$$

The conditional ratio can be written as

$$
\mathcal R_{\mathrm{Lang}}
=
\frac{
\left(\frac{\zeta_1}{K(\tau_1)}\right)^{1/4}
\mathfrak m(\zeta_1)
\mathfrak C_c
\exp\left(
\pi\int_{\zeta_c}^{\zeta_1}
\mathfrak m(\xi)^2|\Psi_B(\xi)|\,d\xi
\right)
}{
T_{n,\alpha,\beta}
}.
$$

This is the version I recommend for A4 implementation because it avoids differentiating $\zeta$ at $\tau_1$.

At $u_1$, the critical-point condition $H_\tau(\tau_1)=0$ implies

$$
W'(\zeta_1)
=
\frac{\zeta_{\tau\tau}(\tau_1)}
{2\zeta_\tau(\tau_1)^2}
W(\zeta_1).
$$

This relation may sharpen the coefficient estimate in a later round, because it restricts the possible Airy phase at $\zeta_1$. I do not use it in the current bound; ignoring it makes the theorem weaker but safer.

Potential gaps:

1. The theorem above starts at $\zeta_c>0$, not at the endpoint or the turning point. Therefore it does not solve the Airy initialization problem. The cut coefficient $\mathfrak C_c$ is exact but unbounded globally.

2. The Airy modulus weight

$$
\mathfrak m(\zeta)^2=\operatorname{Ai}(-\zeta)^2+\operatorname{Bi}(-\zeta)^2
$$

is explicit but not sharp. A DGS weight may be smaller. If $\mathcal V_A$ is too large, this theorem may fail even if the conjecture is true.

3. The formula for $\Psi_B(0)$ has been derived, but a CAS verification log should still be generated. This is exactly the kind of removable-singularity formula where a sign error would poison all subsequent estimates.

4. The first critical point $u_1$ is not algebraically trivial for general $n$. The conditional theorem assumes $u_1$ is isolated and correctly identified. A4’s interval certificate must include root isolation.

5. If $\zeta_1\le\zeta_c$, the selected cut is too far into the first lobe. The test must verify $\zeta_c<\zeta_1$.

6. The KKT first-lobe problem may be sharp near $\theta=0$ and moderate $\alpha$. If $\mathfrak C_c$ is close to the forbidden-side Bi component, a naive allowed-side norm may be too lossy.

7. This approach still needs a gamma-ratio or polynomial normalization envelope indirectly through $\mathfrak C_c$. The normalization is hidden inside $H(u_c)$ and $H_\tau(u_c)$.

Counterexample or obstruction search:

1. **Cutoff obstruction.** If $\zeta_c$ is chosen very small, then $\mathfrak C_c$ may be benign, but the turning-layer computation becomes delicate. If $\zeta_c$ is chosen large, $\mathcal V_A$ shrinks but $\mathfrak C_c$ must propagate through more of the true solution before being measured. This tradeoff is real. The optimal cut is a numerical and analytic parameter, not a formal detail.

2. **Fixed-$\alpha$ obstruction.** The Round 20 warning remains: on the Laguerre face with fixed or bounded $\alpha$, the Langer residual does not necessarily decay as in the bulk. The allowed-side theorem here will likely not close small $\alpha$ uniformly. That is why the rational-Bessel or Riccati track should remain active.

3. **Bulk falsification mechanism.** If, on the proposed box

n=100,\qquad

A4 proves $\inf\mathcal R_{\mathrm{Lang}}>1$, then the present Airy-envelope bulk theorem is too weak. This would not disprove KKT, but it would falsify this particular proof route.

Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration:

### Alternative 1: Rational-coordinate Bessel track for small $\alpha$

For $\alpha\le C\sqrt n$, the hard-edge singularity should be modeled by a Bessel equation rather than by a bulk Airy equation. The rational coordinate should be denoted

$$
z=\frac{Bu}{B-u}
$$

to avoid conflict with Riccati notation. The known exact normal form is

$$
Y_z''+\frac{K_B(u(z))+1/4}{z^2}Y_z=0,
\qquad
Y_z=z^{1/2}H.
$$

A plausible Bessel reference equation should preserve the singular term and use a model of the form

$$
Y_{\mathrm{ref}}''+
\left(
\frac{\Lambda_{\mathrm{ref}}}{z}
+
\frac{1/4-\alpha^2}{z^2}
\right)
Y_{\mathrm{ref}}=0
$$

or an equivalent $J_\alpha(2\sqrt{\Lambda_{\mathrm{ref}}z})$ normalization. The exact residual must be rederived; the previously quoted

$$
\Delta Q(z)
=
-\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z}
-
\frac{\Delta_BB^2}{(B+z)^2}
$$

should not be trusted until it is tied to a fully specified reference equation.

What would make this route work: a Volterra kernel bound using Bessel moduli showing

$$
\mathcal V_{\mathrm{Bess}}\le C\frac{\alpha^3}{n^2}
$$

or a corrected version with constants, on $\alpha\le C\sqrt n$.

What would falsify it: if the exact Bessel-kernel integral has a leading term of order $\alpha^2/n$ or a non-decaying logarithmic term on the Laguerre face.

### Alternative 2: Riccati monotonicity and interval IVP

Let

$$
R(u)=p_B(u)\frac{H'(u)}{H(u)}.
$$

Then

$$
p_B R'(u)+R(u)^2+K_B(u)=0.
$$

The endpoint expansion

$$
R(u)=\frac{\alpha}{2}+uw(u)
$$

has

$$
w(0)=-\frac{\Lambda_B}{\alpha+1}.
$$

This route is attractive because it avoids oscillatory phase until the first zero of $H$ and can be initialized rigorously at $u=0$. It may be especially effective for $n=1,2$ and for interval certificates.

What would make it work: a sign-stable Riccati comparison proving that $R$ crosses $0$ only once in the cap and that $H$ at that crossing obeys the target.

What would falsify it: multiple Riccati turns or loss of sign control before the first critical point in the residual strip.

For $n=2$, A3 must finalize the corrected cubic. Once the cubic is stable in $\theta$, interval Newton can isolate all critical points.

What would make this route work: exact outward-rounded evaluation of
