Summary:

Round 22 A1 result: I do **not** close the KKT conjecture. I make concrete progress on the judge-assigned bulk Langer track by reducing the missing Frobenius-to-Airy connection coefficient $\mathfrak C_c$ to explicit one-dimensional quantities.

The new usable object is a one-sided recessive-Airy initialization theorem on the forbidden side of the endpoint turning point. It gives a computable upper bound for $\mathfrak C_c$ in terms of:

1. the exact endpoint Frobenius coefficient $A_F$;
2. a finite renormalized forbidden-side action constant $C_0$;
3. a Volterra initialization functional $E_0(\zeta_c)$;
4. a derivative leakage functional $G_0(\zeta_c)$;
5. the already available allowed-side propagation integral from Round 21.

This does not prove the first-lobe amplitude theorem, because the new functionals still need explicit uniform bounds in the residual strip. But it sharply localizes the missing proof obligation: the Langer track now fails or succeeds according to a displayed scalar inequality, not according to an undefined “Airy matching constant.”

Main claim or direction:

The bulk Langer route should be kept, but only in the following sharpened form.

Let

$$

\qquad
\tau=\log\frac{u}{B-u},

$$
K(\tau)=K_B(u(\tau)),
\qquad
K=\zeta\zeta_\tau^2,
\qquad
H=\zeta_\tau^{-1/2}W,

with $\zeta<0$ on the forbidden side and $\zeta>0$ on the allowed side. Then

$$
W_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta W=\Psi_B(\zeta)W.

Round 21 already gave the allowed-side estimate from a cut $\zeta_c>0$ to the first critical point. The missing object was

\mathfrak C_c=(a_c^2+b_c^2)^{1/2},

the Airy coefficient norm at the cut. I reduce $\mathfrak C_c$ to an explicit endpoint-normalized expression:

\mathfrak C_c

and $E_0,G_0,C_0$ are explicitly defined below.

Detailed reasoning:

## 1. Fixed endpoint notation

Work in the residual right endpoint range

$$
n\ge1,
\qquad
\frac12<\alpha<\alpha_E(n):=\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3},
\qquad
\beta\ge0,
$$

with

$$
B=n+\alpha+\beta+1,
\qquad
u=\frac{B(1-x)}2,
\qquad
H(u)=g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right).
$$

The imported endpoint-cap theorem gives

$$
0\le u\le u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n,
$$

and

$$
(p_BH')'+q_BH=0,
\qquad
p_B(u)=u\left(1-\frac uB\right),
$$

where

$$
K_B(u):=p_B(u)q_B(u)
=
-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+\Lambda_Bu-\Delta_Bu^2.
$$

The first turning point $u_0$ is the smaller positive root of $K_B$ when it lies in the cap:

$$
u_0=
\frac{\Lambda_B-\sqrt{\Lambda_B^2-\Delta_B\alpha^2}}{2\Delta_B}.
$$

The dynamic variable is

$$
\tau=\log\frac{u}{B-u},
\qquad
u(\tau)=\frac{Be^\tau}{1+e^\tau},
\qquad
\frac{du}{d\tau}=p_B(u).
$$

Thus

$$
H_{\tau\tau}+K_B(u(\tau))H=0.
$$

All statements in this subsection are imported certified algebra, not new claims.

## 2. Langer map and sign convention

Define

$$
K(\tau)=K_B(u(\tau)).
$$

On the forbidden side $\tau<\tau_0$, $K<0$. On the allowed side $\tau>\tau_0$, $K>0$. Use the standard Langer coordinate determined by

$$
K(\tau)=\zeta(\tau)\zeta_\tau(\tau)^2,
$$

with

$$
\zeta(\tau)<0\quad(\tau<\tau_0),
\qquad
\zeta(\tau_0)=0,
\qquad
\zeta(\tau)>0\quad(\tau>\tau_0).
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\frac23(-\zeta)^{3/2}
=
\int_{\tau}^{\tau_0}\sqrt{-K(s)}\,ds
\quad(\tau<\tau_0),
$$

and

$$
\frac23\zeta^{3/2}
=
\int_{\tau_0}^{\tau}\sqrt{K(s)}\,ds
\quad(\tau>\tau_0).
$$

Set

$$
H(\tau)=\zeta_\tau(\tau)^{-1/2}W(\zeta).
$$

Then

$$
W_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta W=\Psi_B(\zeta)W,
$$

where, away from $\zeta=0$,

$$
\Psi_B(\zeta)
=
\frac{\zeta K_{\tau\tau}}{4K^2}
-
\frac{5\zeta K_\tau^2}{16K^3}
+
\frac{5}{16\zeta^2}.
$$

This is the Round 21 residual formula. I treat it as certified away from $\zeta=0$, with the removable value at $\zeta=0$ still requiring permanent CAS-style archival verification.

## 3. Exact Frobenius normalization at $u=0$

The regular endpoint branch has

$$
H(u)\sim A_F u^{\alpha/2}
\qquad
(u\downarrow0),
$$

where

$$
A_F
=
B^{-\alpha/2}
\frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha+1)}
\left(
\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(B)}
{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(B-\alpha)}
\right)^{1/2}.
$$

This coefficient is imported from the A3 algebra audit.

Define the forbidden-side action

$$
I(u)
=
\int_u^{u_0}
\frac{\sqrt{-K_B(t)}}{p_B(t)}\,dt.
$$

Since

$$
K_B(u)=-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+O(u),
\qquad
p_B(u)=u+O(u^2),
$$

we have

$$
I(u)=\frac{\alpha}{2}\log\frac1u+O(1).
$$

Define the finite renormalized action constant

$$
C_0
=
\lim_{u\downarrow0}
\left[
I(u)+\frac{\alpha}{2}\log u
\right].
$$

Equivalently,

$$
C_0
=
\int_0^{u_0}
\left(
\frac{\sqrt{-K_B(t)}}{p_B(t)}
-
\frac{\alpha}{2t}
\right)\,dt
+
\frac{\alpha}{2}\log u_0.
$$

This formula is new in this round and is useful because it removes the singularity explicitly.

## 4. The endpoint Airy coefficient at $-\infty$

Let

$$
\mathcal A(\zeta)=\operatorname{Ai}(-\zeta),
\qquad
\mathcal B(\zeta)=\operatorname{Bi}(-\zeta).
$$

For $\zeta\to-\infty$, $\mathcal A(\zeta)=\operatorname{Ai}(-\zeta)$ is recessive. Since

$$
\frac23(-\zeta)^{3/2}=I(u),
$$

the Airy asymptotic gives

$$
\mathcal A(\zeta)
\sim
\frac{1}{2\sqrt{\pi}}(-\zeta)^{-1/4}e^{-I(u)}.
$$

Also

$$
|\zeta_\tau|
=
\frac{\sqrt{-K}}{(-\zeta)^{1/2}},
$$

so

$$
H
=
\zeta_\tau^{-1/2}W
\sim
\frac{\mathfrak C_-}{2\sqrt{\pi}}
(-K)^{-1/4}e^{-I(u)}
\qquad
(\zeta\to-\infty)
$$

if

$$
W(\zeta)\sim \mathfrak C_-\mathcal A(\zeta).
$$

As $u\downarrow0$,

$$
(-K)^{-1/4}\to\left(\frac{\alpha^2}{4}\right)^{-1/4}
=
\left(\frac{2}{\alpha}\right)^{1/2},
$$

and

$$
e^{-I(u)}\sim e^{-C_0}u^{\alpha/2}.
$$

Matching with $H(u)\sim A_Fu^{\alpha/2}$ yields the exact nominal recessive Airy coefficient

$$
\boxed{
\mathfrak C_-
=
\sqrt{2\pi\alpha}\,A_F e^{C_0}.
}
$$

Status: proved, conditional only on the standard Airy asymptotic and the Langer residual equation. This is the main new concrete formula of this response.

## 5. Recessive Volterra theorem from $-\infty$ to a positive cut

The coefficient $\mathfrak C_-$ is not yet $\mathfrak C_c$, because $\Psi_B$ perturbs the Airy equation before the cut. To control the perturbation without the divergent full Airy envelope on the forbidden side, use a one-sided recessive quotient.

Let

$$
W(\zeta)=\mathfrak C_-\mathcal A(\zeta)y(\zeta).
$$

Then

$$
W_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta W=\Psi_BW
$$

and

$$
\mathcal A_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta\mathcal A=0
$$

imply

$$
(\mathcal A^2y')'=\Psi_B\mathcal A^2y,
$$

with endpoint condition

$$
y(-\infty)=1,\qquad y'(-\infty)=0.
$$

Fix a cut

$$
0<\zeta_c<j_{\operatorname{Ai},1},
$$

where $j_{\operatorname{Ai},1}\approx2.3381$ is the first positive zero of $\operatorname{Ai}(-\zeta)$ as a function of $\zeta$. This ensures $\mathcal A$ has no zero on $(-\infty,\zeta_c]$.

Define

$$
E_0(\zeta_c)
=
\int_{-\infty}^{\zeta_c}
|\Psi_B(t)|\mathcal A(t)^2
\left[
\int_t^{\zeta_c}\mathcal A(s)^{-2}\,ds
\right]dt,
$$

and

$$
G_0(\zeta_c)
=
\int_{-\infty}^{\zeta_c}
|\Psi_B(t)|\mathcal A(t)^2\,dt.
$$

Then the Volterra equation gives

$$
|y(\zeta_c)|\le e^{E_0(\zeta_c)},
$$

and

$$
|y'(\zeta_c)|
\le
\mathcal A(\zeta_c)^{-2}
G_0(\zeta_c)e^{E_0(\zeta_c)}.
$$

The integrals are finite. The key point is that as $\zeta\to-\infty$, $\Psi_B(\zeta)\sim 5/(16\zeta^2)$ up to faster-decaying corrections, while the product

$$
\mathcal A(t)^2\int_t^{\zeta_c}\mathcal A(s)^{-2}\,ds
$$

has only algebraic growth of order $O(|t|^{-1/2})$. Thus the $E_0$ integrand is $O(|t|^{-5/2})$.

Status: proved as a Volterra bound, assuming the stated asymptotic of $\Psi_B$; the asymptotic should be added to A3’s symbolic-certificate list.

## 6. Explicit bound for $\mathfrak C_c$

At the cut, write

$$
W(\zeta_c)=a_c\mathcal A(\zeta_c)+b_c\mathcal B(\zeta_c),
$$

$$
W'(\zeta_c)=a_c\mathcal A'(\zeta_c)+b_c\mathcal B'(\zeta_c).
$$

The Wronskian satisfies

$$
|\mathcal A\mathcal B'-\mathcal A'\mathcal B|=\frac1\pi.
$$

Using

$$
W=\mathfrak C_-\mathcal A y,
\qquad
W'=\mathfrak C_-(\mathcal A' y+\mathcal A y'),
$$

one gets

$$
|b_c|
\le
\pi\mathfrak C_- \mathcal A_c^2 |y'_c|,
$$

and

$$
|a_c|
\le
\mathfrak C_-|y_c|
+
\pi\mathfrak C_-|\mathcal A_c\mathcal B_c|\,|y'_c|.
$$

Insert the Volterra bounds to obtain

$$
\boxed{
\mathfrak C_c
\le
\mathfrak C_-
e^{E_0}
\left[
\left(1+\pi\left|\frac{\mathcal B_c}{\mathcal A_c}\right|G_0\right)^2
+
(\pi G_0)^2
\right]^{1/2}.
}
$$

Here

$$
\mathcal A_c=\mathcal A(\zeta_c),
\qquad
\mathcal B_c=\mathcal B(\zeta_c),
\qquad
E_0=E_0(\zeta_c),
\qquad
G_0=G_0(\zeta_c).
$$

This is the requested Frobenius-to-Airy connection bound. It is explicit and finite. It still requires uniform estimates for $C_0,E_0,G_0$.
