The King’s College record states that Landau proves, among other things, that $\sup_x |J_\nu(x)|$ strictly decreases from $1$ to $0$ as $\nu$ increases from $0$ to $\infty$. This supports reducing $\sup_{\nu\ge1/2,t\ge0}|J_\nu(t)|$ to the half-order case, but it does not itself compute the half-order maximum.

  $$
  J_{1/2}(t)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi t}}\sin t,
  $$
  and positive critical points satisfy $\tan t=2t$. Thus the first maximum is approximately $0.6791921047$, not $2/\pi$. This remains consistent with Landau’s monotonicity result.
- Wendel/Gautschi gamma-ratio support is partially web-verified through a secondary reference: the Gautschi inequality page records Wendel’s 1948 inequality
  $$
  \left(\frac{x}{x+s}\right)^{1-s}
  \le
  \frac{\Gamma(x+s)}{x^s\Gamma(x)}
  \le 1,
  \qquad x>0,\quad 0<s<1,
  $$

A4:

A4’s most valuable contribution is the near-certificate for the full $n=1$ residual strip. The method is simple and promising.

For $n=1$,
$$
B=\alpha+\beta+2,
\qquad
P_1^{(\alpha,\beta)}\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)=\alpha+1-u,
\qquad
0\le u\le u_\sigma=1.
$$
A4 uses
$$
\frac{\Gamma(B)}{\Gamma(B-\alpha)}\le B^\alpha
$$
and $(1-u/B)^\beta\le1$ to obtain
$$
H_1(u)^2
\le
\frac{1}{\Gamma(\alpha+2)}
u^\alpha(\alpha+1-u)^2.
$$
The scalar envelope
$$
f(u)=u^\alpha(\alpha+1-u)^2
$$
has its critical point at
$$
u_*=\frac{\alpha(\alpha+1)}{\alpha+2},
$$
which lies in $[0,1]$ for $\alpha\in[1/2,6/5]$. Thus
$$
\max_{0\le u\le1} f(u)
=
\frac{4\alpha^\alpha(\alpha+1)^{\alpha+2}}{(\alpha+2)^{\alpha+2}}.
$$
This reduces the $n=1$ proof to a one-variable scalar bound:
$$
E(\alpha)
=
\left(
\frac{
4\alpha^\alpha(\alpha+1)^{\alpha+2}
}{
(\alpha+2)^{\alpha+2}\Gamma(\alpha+2)
}
\right)^2
<
\frac{2}{\alpha+2}
\le
T_{1,\alpha,\beta}^4.
$$

The proof idea is excellent, but A4’s numerical constants are slightly wrong. Direct evaluation gives
$$
E(6/5)\approx 0.383396,
$$
not $<0.380$. This does not threaten the result, because the limiting target lower bound is
$$
\frac{2}{6/5+2}=\frac58=0.625.
$$
The available margin is still large. The certificate should be repaired by proving, for example,
$$
E(\alpha)<0.384
\qquad
\text{or simply}
\qquad
E(\alpha)<0.39
$$
on $[1/2,6/5]$ with outward-rounded interval arithmetic or elementary gamma/trigamma bounds.

A4’s convexity argument is plausible but not yet formally certified. It uses the lower bound
$$

$$
for
$$
h(\alpha)=\frac12\log E(\alpha),
$$
but the trigamma estimate $\psi'(2.5)\approx0.4904$ is numerical, not an outward-rounded proof. This is easy to fix in the next round by bounding the series
$$

with an explicit rational tail estimate.
