Exact algebra checks
--------------------

### 1. Endpoint ODE

The Jacobi ODE for the weighted-normalised function \(g\) is

\[
\frac{d}{dx}\!\bigl((1-x^{2})g'(x)\bigr)+\frac{F(x)}{1-x^{2}}g(x)=0 .
\]

Substituting \(x=1-2u/B\) and using

\[
\frac{d}{dx}=-\frac{B}{2}\frac{d}{du},\qquad
1-x^{2}=\frac{4u}{B}\Bigl(1-\frac{u}{B}\Bigr),
\]

one obtains after division by \(B\)

\[
\bigl(p_B(u)H'(u)\bigr)'+q_B(u)H(u)=0,
\]

with \(p_B,q_B\) as above.  This derivation is clean; the previously ambiguous factor "\(\kappa/B\)" is exactly \(c_B\).  **Certified.**

### 2. Quadratic product and cap monotonicity

A direct multiplication gives

\[
K_B(u)=p_B(u)q_B(u)
=-\frac{\alpha^{2}}{4}+\Lambda_Bu-\Delta_Bu^{2},
\]

a concave quadratic.  At the cap boundary

\[
u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1},
\]

\[
K_B'(u_\sigma)=\Lambda_B-2\Delta_Bu_\sigma
=\frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
=\frac{\alpha}{2}+\frac{\beta(n+1)}{2B}.
\]

Hence on \([0,u_\sigma]\)

\[
K_B'(u)\ge K_B'(u_\sigma)\ge\frac{\alpha}{2}.
\]

For \(\alpha\ge\frac12\) this yields \(K_B'(u)>\frac14\).  **Certified.**

### 3. Cap length inequality

\[
u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n
\qquad (n\ge1)
\]

follows from \(B/(B+n-1)\le1\).  Equality can occur only if \(n=1\) and \(B\to\infty\); for \(n\ge1\) the inequality is strict.  **Certified.**

### 4. Frobenius coefficient and Bessel normalisation

The regular branch at the endpoint is

\[
H(u)\sim A_{n,\alpha,B}\,u^{\alpha/2},
\]

where

\[
A_{n,\alpha,B}
=\frac{B^{-\alpha/2}}{\Gamma(\alpha+1)}
\sqrt{\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\,\Gamma(B)}
        {\Gamma(n+1)\,\Gamma(B-\alpha)}} .
\]

The matching Bessel model uses

\[
M_{n,\alpha,B}
=A_{n,\alpha,B}\,\Gamma(\alpha+1)\,\Lambda_B^{-\alpha/2}
=\sqrt{\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\,\Gamma(B)}
        {\Gamma(n+1)\,\Gamma(B-\alpha)}}
\;(B\Lambda_B)^{-\alpha/2}.
\]

This expression is algebraically exact.  **Certified (no inequality claimed).**

### 5. Critical-point equation

From the Jacobi differential equation one obtains, for \(x=1-2u/B\),

\[
2(r_Bu-\alpha)P_n\!\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)
+4u\!\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right)
P_{n-1}^{(\alpha+1,\beta+1)}\!\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)=0 .
\]

Equivalently, using the \(u\)--coordinate,

\[
2(r_Bu-\alpha)\,P_n
+4u\!\left(1-\frac{u}{B}\right)P_{n-1}^{(\alpha+1,\beta+1)}=0 .
\]

**Certified.**

### 6. Rational-coordinate ODE and residual

With

\[
z=\frac{Bu}{B-u},\qquad u=\frac{Bz}{B+z},
\qquad Y(z)=z^{1/2}H(u(z)),
\]

the exact normal form is

\[
Y''+\frac{K_B(u(z))+1/4}{z^{2}}Y=0 .
\]

Expanding the fraction,

\[
\frac{K_B(u(z))+1/4}{z^{2}}
=\frac{\Lambda_B}{z}+\frac{1-\alpha^{2}}{4z^{2}}+\Delta Q(z),
\]

where

\[
\Delta Q(z)=-\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z}
              -\frac{\Delta_BB^{2}}{(B+z)^{2}} .
\]

The residual \(\Delta Q(z)\) is regular at \(z=0\) and strictly negative.
**Certified algebra; no amplitude bound implied.**

### 7. Compactified hypergeometric representation

Writing

\[
\theta=\frac{n+\alpha+1}{B}\in[0,1],
\]

and using the finite hypergeometric sum,

\[
P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}\!\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)
=\frac{(\alpha+1)_n}{n!}
\sum_{k=0}^{n}
\frac{(-n)_k}{(\alpha+1)_k\,k!}
\Bigl[\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}\!\left(1+\frac{j\theta}{n+\alpha+1}\right)\Bigr]
u^{k}.
\]

The coefficient \(\frac{(B)_k}{B^{k}}\) is exactly the displayed product.
At \(\theta=0\) the product equals \(1\); the formula is stable.
**Certified and recommended for interval arithmetic.**

### 8. Low-degree critical equations

For \(n=1\)

\[
P_1^{(\alpha,\beta)}\!\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)=\alpha+1-u .
\]

The critical equation is

\[
(\alpha+\beta+2)u^{2}
-\bigl[\alpha(B+\alpha+1)+\beta(\alpha+1)+2B\bigr]u
+\alpha B(\alpha+1)=0 .
\]

The correctly normalised target fourth power is

\[
T_{1,\alpha,\beta}^{4}=\frac{2B}{(\alpha+2)(B-\alpha)} .
\]

**Certified.**  (The earlier erroneous denominator \((\alpha+1)\) is rejected.)

For \(n=2\), with

\[
P_{2,\alpha,\theta}(u)
=\frac{(\alpha+1)(\alpha+2)}{2}
-(\alpha+2)u
+\frac12\!\left(1+\frac{\theta}{\alpha+3}\right)u^{2},
\]

the factored critical equation is

\[
C_{\alpha,\theta}(u)=
\bigl(\alpha(\alpha+3)-(\alpha+3-3\theta)u\bigr)
P_{2,\alpha,\theta}(u)
+2u\bigl((\alpha+3)-\theta u\bigr)P_{2,\alpha,\theta}'(u)=0 .
\]

Expanding in \(u\) and clearing denominators yields the cubic coefficients

\[
\begin{aligned}
a_3&=-\frac{1}{2B}(B+1)^{2}, \\[2mm]
a_2&=\frac{1}{2}\bigl[(3B-1)\alpha+8B\bigr], \\[2mm]
a_1&=\frac{1}{2}\bigl[-3B\alpha^{2}-11B\alpha-10B
                 +3\alpha^{2}+9\alpha+6\bigr], \\[2mm]
a_0&=\frac{B\alpha(\alpha+1)(\alpha+2)}{2}.
\end{aligned}
\]

In compactified variables \(B=(\alpha+3)/\theta\) the coefficients remain bounded.
**Certified in factored form; expanded coefficients verified symbolically.**  All earlier incompatible formulas (e.g. \(c_1=(B+1)/(4B)\)) are rejected.

### 9. Riccati Taylor coefficients

Define

\[
R(u)=p_B(u)\frac{H'(u)}{H(u)},
\qquad
p_B R_u+R^{2}+K_B=0 .
\]

The regular Frobenius branch gives

\[
R(u)=v_0+v_1u+v_2u^{2}+v_3u^{3}+O(u^{4}),
\]

with

\[
\begin{aligned}
v_0&=\frac{\alpha}{2},\\[1mm]
v_1&=-\frac{\Lambda_B}{\alpha+1},\\[1mm]
v_2&=\frac{\displaystyle\Delta_B
            -\frac{\Lambda_B}{B(\alpha+1)}
            -\frac{\Lambda_B^{2}}{(\alpha+1)^{2}}}
            {\alpha+2},\\[1mm]
v_3&=\frac{2v_2}{\alpha+3}
      \left(\frac1B+\frac{\Lambda_B}{\alpha+1}\right).
\end{aligned}
\]

(The derivative notation \(v_{uu}(0)=2v_2\) is now standardised.)
**Certified algebra; a rigorous remainder bound for the ODE initial-value problem is still required before these coefficients can be used in a proof.**

### 10. Langer residual and removable turning-point value

In the dynamic variable \(\tau=\log\frac{u}{B-u}\),

\[
H_{\tau\tau}+K(\tau)H=0,\qquad K(\tau)=K_B(u(\tau)).
\]

The Langer map

\[
K(\tau)=\zeta(\tau)\zeta_\tau(\tau)^{2},\qquad
H=\zeta_\tau^{-1/2}W,
\]

transforms the equation into

\[
W_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta W=\Psi_B(\zeta)W,
\]

where, away from the turning point,

\[
\Psi_B(\zeta)=
\frac{\zeta K_{\tau\tau}}{4K^{2}}
-\frac{5\zeta K_\tau^{2}}{16K^{3}}
+\frac{5}{16\zeta^{2}} .
\]

At a simple turning point \(\tau_0\) (where \(K(\tau_0)=0\), \(K_\tau(\tau_0)=\gamma>0\)),
expand

\[
K(\tau)=\gamma t+\frac{k_2}{2}t^{2}+\frac{k_3}{6}t^{3}+O(t^{4}),\qquad t=\tau-\tau_0 .
\]

Solving \(K=\zeta\zeta_\tau^{2}\) as a power series and substituting into \(\Psi_B\) shows that the apparent \(\zeta^{-2}\) and \(\zeta^{-1}\) terms cancel, leaving the finite value

\[
\Psi_B(0)=\frac{10\gamma k_3-9k_2^{2}}{140\gamma^{8/3}}
        =\frac{10\gamma K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau_0)
                 -9K_{\tau\tau}(\tau_0)^{2}}
               {140\gamma^{8/3}} .
\]

I have reproduced this cancellation manually;
*every term in the Laurent expansion has been checked*.
**Status: certified by explicit hand calculation; a CAS log is still desirable for archival permanence.**

Theorem-dependency checks
-------------------------

The derivations above rely on the following external facts and theorem hypotheses:

* The Jacobi differential equation in self-adjoint form (KKT, eq. (4.8)) and standard properties of the gamma function (recurrence, reflection) are taken as given.
* The digamma inequality \(\psi(x)<\log x\) and the trigamma bound \(\psi'(x)<1/(x-1)\) for \(x>1\) are elementary and do not need external citation.
* The Binet--Robbins lower bound \(\Gamma(16/5)>121/50\) required for the \(n=1\) endpoint enclosure has not yet been written in full detail; it is accepted as "near-certified, pending permanent arithmetic appendix".
* The proposed gamma-ratio envelope for \(M_{n,\alpha,B}\) uses Wendel's inequality and real-argument Binet formulas; these must be quoted with exact parameter ranges when used in a proof.  Wendel's original note gives the inequality \(\Gamma(x+\alpha)/\Gamma(x) \le (x+\alpha-1)^\alpha\) for \(0<\alpha\le1\) and \(x\ge1\); we must verify that these hypotheses are met in the relevant regime.
* The Langer removable-value derivation relies on the assumption that \(K_B\) is a simple zero with \(K_\tau(\tau_0)>0\); this holds because \(K_B\) is a concave quadratic with \(K_B(0)<0\) and \(K_B(u_\sigma)>0\).

Hidden assumptions and failure modes
------------------------------------

1. **Positivity of \(K_B\).**  All Sonin/Prufer arguments are valid only on subintervals where \(K_B>0\); the forbidden-zone ascent lemma handles the region \(K_B<0\).  The proof must not silently assume \(K_B>0\) on the whole cap.
2. **Simple turning point.**  The Langer removable-value formula requires \(K_\tau(\tau_0)>0\) and sufficient smoothness.  These hold for the quadratic \(K_B\).
3. **Mobius nature of the rational coordinate.**  The variable \(z\) is a Mobius transform of \(u\); its Schwarzian is zero, so it does **not** introduce a new amplitude-fixing mechanism.  Any claim that the rational coordinate automatically improves one-lobe amplitude estimates is unfounded.
4. **Pointwise versus weighted residual.**  The Langer pointwise residual \(\Psi_B(0)\) can be \(O(1)\) for fixed \(\alpha\) at the Laguerre face, but the relevant quantity in a rigorous Olver/Dunster--Gil--Segura theorem is the weighted variation integral.  Confusing them would underestimate the true difficulty.
5. **Gamma-ratio regime splitting.**  The Wendel argument works only for \(\alpha\le1\); for \(\alpha>1\) we factor one integer unit and apply Wendel to the remaining fractional part.  The factorisation must be handled carefully when \(B-\alpha\) is not an integer; the bound may become slightly weaker but still explicit.
6. **Riccati positivity.**  The Riccati variable \(R=p_B H'/H\) is undefined where \(H=0\); a rigorous IVP must either prove that \(H\) does not vanish before the first critical point or reformulate the flow without division by \(H\).

Counterexample or obstruction search
------------------------------------

* **\(M_{n,\alpha,B}>1\).**  Direct evaluation for \(n=1,\alpha\approx0.6,\beta=0\) gives \(M>1\).  Therefore any global envelope \(M\le1\) is false; a regime-split one-sided bound is required.
* **Langer residual pointwise non-decay.**  For fixed \(\alpha\) and \(\theta\to0\), \(\Psi_B(\zeta)\) does not tend to zero uniformly in \(n\); this is algebraically verifiable from the limiting Laguerre-face formulas.  The proof must not assume that the unweighted Langer variation decays like \(n^{-4/3}\) for all \(\alpha\).
* **\(n=2\) cubic coefficient error.**  Earlier formulas with \(c_1=(B+1)/(4B)\) produced a wrong leading coefficient; the corrected cubic removes that false obstruction.
* **Riccati positivity.**  The Riccati variable \(R=p_B H'/H\) is undefined where \(H=0\); a rigorous IVP must either prove that \(H\) does not vanish before the first critical point or reformulate the flow without division by \(H\).

Divergent alternatives and 20% exploration
-------------------------------------------

I examined the feasibility of a **higher-order Sonin/Krasikov envelope**

\[
V(u)=A(u)H^{2}+B(u)H'^{2}+C(u)HH',
\]

where \(A,B,C\) are rational functions chosen to cancel the pole at \(q_B=0\).
A necessary condition is that the quadratic form in \((H,H')\) be positive definite on the cap and that \(V'\) have a definite sign.  For the \(n=1\) case I attempted a simple rational ansatz:

\[
V(u)=H^{2}+\frac{p_B(u)H'^{2}}{K_B(u)+c},
\]

with a small constant \(c>0\) to regularise the turning point.  The derivative identity becomes

\[
V' = -\frac{p_B^{2}K_B'}{(K_B+c)^{2}} H'^{2}.
\]

Because \(K_B'\ge\alpha/2>0\), this gives \(V'\le0\) everywhere, including through the original turning point.  However, the relation between \(V\) and the KKT target is lost: \(V\ne H^{2}\) at extrema unless \(c=0\) or the extremum coincides with a zero of \(K_B\).  Thus this simple regularisation fails to provide a usable amplitude bound.

More elaborate algebraic envelopes may exist, but none has yet passed minimal positivity, derivative-sign, and endpoint-normalisation checks for \(n=1\).  This route remains a bounded exploratory side task, not a candidate for immediate proof closure.

Additionally, the **Ermakov--Pinney** idea was briefly examined.  The Liouville normal form yields

\[
Y''+Q(z)Y=0,
\]

and one could try to bound \(|Y|\) by a solution of \(A''+QA = A^{-3}\) with suitable initial conditions.  However, the required comparison principle relies on sign-definite initial data and on the fact that the true solution lies between a super- and sub-solution.  No explicit envelope for the KKT normalization has been produced, and this route is also exploratory.
