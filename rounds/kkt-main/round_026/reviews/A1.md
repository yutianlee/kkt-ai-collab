## A2

A2's most valuable contribution is making the rational-Bessel route more quantitative. The core setup is the rational coordinate

$$
z=\frac{Bu}{B-u},
\qquad
Y=z^{1/2}H,
$$

with normal form

$$
Y''+
\left(
\frac{\Lambda_B}{z}
+
\frac{1-\alpha^2}{4z^2}
+
\Delta Q(z)
\right)Y=0,
$$

where

$$
\Delta Q(z)
=
-\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z}
-
\frac{\Delta_BB^2}{(B+z)^2}.
$$

A2 then uses

$$
W_1(z)=\sqrt z\,J_\alpha(2\sqrt{\Lambda_Bz}),
\qquad
Y=hW_1,
$$

which gives the relative-amplitude identity

$$
(W_1^2h')'=-\Delta Q\,W_1^2h
=
|\Delta Q|W_1^2h
$$

on intervals where $W_1>0$. This is a genuinely useful sign-definite identity. It avoids the old irregular $Y_\alpha$ kernel and should remain in the proof program.

A2's bound

$$
\int_0^y W_1(x)^2\,dx
\le
\frac y2 W_1(y)^2
$$

before the first Bessel stationary point is also useful. It leads to the conditional variation estimate

$$
\mathcal V_{\mathrm{Bess}}(z_0)
\le
\frac{M_Q(j'_{\alpha,1})^4}{64\Lambda_B^2},
\qquad
z_0=\frac{(j'_{\alpha,1})^2}{4\Lambda_B}.
$$

This should be recorded as a conditional lemma with the explicit hypothesis

$$
2\sqrt{\Lambda_B z}\le j'_{\alpha,1}.
$$

A2 also correctly emphasizes that $\Delta Q(0)=O(1)$ is not automatically fatal: the Bessel-core weight can suppress the hard-edge residual in the Volterra integral. This is the right distinction between pointwise residual size and weighted variation.

## A3

A3's output is the algebraic anchor of the round.

The following identities should be adopted, subject to final copyediting:

1. Endpoint equation:

$$
(p_BH')'+q_BH=0,
\qquad
p_B(u)=u\left(1-\frac uB\right),
$$

$$
q_B(u)=
c_B-\frac{(r_Bu-\alpha)^2}{4u(1-u/B)},
$$

where

$$
r_B=1-\frac{n+1}{B},
\qquad
c_B=n+\frac12-\frac{n+1}{2B}.
$$

2. Quadratic Sonin product:

$$
K_B(u)=p_Bq_B
=
-\frac{\alpha^2}{4}+\Lambda_Bu-\Delta_Bu^2,
$$

with

$$
\Lambda_B=c_B+\frac{\alpha r_B}{2},
\qquad
\Delta_B=\frac{c_B}{B}+\frac{r_B^2}{4}.
$$

3. Cap length and monotonicity:

$$
u_\sigma=\frac{nB}{B+n-1}\le n,
$$

$$
K_B'(u_\sigma)
=
\frac{(\alpha+\beta)(n+\alpha+1)}{2B}
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}+\frac{\beta(n+1)}{2B}.
$$

Thus, in the residual strip $\alpha>1/2$,

$$
K_B'(u)>\frac14
$$

on the cap.

4. Frobenius and Bessel normalization:

$$
H(u)\sim A_{n,\alpha,B}u^{\alpha/2},
$$

$$
A_{n,\alpha,B}
=
\frac{B^{-\alpha/2}}{\Gamma(\alpha+1)}
\sqrt{
\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(B)}
{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(B-\alpha)}
},
$$

and

$$
M_{n,\alpha,B}
=
\sqrt{
\frac{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(B)}
{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(B-\alpha)}
}
(B\Lambda_B)^{-\alpha/2}.
$$

5. Rational-coordinate residual:

$$
\frac{K_B(u(z))+1/4}{z^2}
=
\frac{\Lambda_B}{z}
+
\frac{1-\alpha^2}{4z^2}
+
\Delta Q(z),
$$

$$
\Delta Q(z)
=
-\frac{\Lambda_B}{B+z}
-
\frac{\Delta_BB^2}{(B+z)^2}.
$$

6. Stable compactified hypergeometric representation:

$$
P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}\left(1-\frac{2u}{B}\right)
=
\frac{(\alpha+1)_n}{n!}
\sum_{k=0}^{n}
\frac{(-n)_k}{(\alpha+1)_k k!}
\left[
\prod_{j=0}^{k-1}
\left(
1+\frac{j\theta}{n+\alpha+1}
\right)
\right]u^k,
$$

where

$$
\theta=\frac{n+\alpha+1}{B}.
$$

7. Correct $n=2$ factored critical equation:

$$
P_{2,\alpha,\theta}(u)
=
\frac{(\alpha+1)(\alpha+2)}2
-(\alpha+2)u
+
\frac12\left(1+\frac{\theta}{\alpha+3}\right)u^2,
$$

$$
C_{\alpha,\theta}(u)=
\left(
\alpha(\alpha+3)-(\alpha+3-3\theta)u
\right)
P_{2,\alpha,\theta}(u)
+
2u\left((\alpha+3)-\theta u\right)
P_{2,\alpha,\theta}'(u).
$$

The expanded cubic, after multiplication by $-B/2$ in the uncompactified variable convention, is consistent with direct expansion:

$$
a_3=-\frac{(B+1)^2}{2B},
$$

$$
a_2=\frac12\left((3B-1)\alpha+8B\right),
$$

$$
a_1=\frac12\left(
-3B\alpha^2-11B\alpha-10B+3\alpha^2+9\alpha+6
\right),
$$

$$
a_0=\frac{B\alpha(\alpha+1)(\alpha+2)}2.
$$

This is implementation-safe if the denominator-clearing convention is recorded.

8. Langer residual and removable turning-point value:

For

$$
H_{\tau\tau}+K(\tau)H=0,
\qquad
K(\tau)=K_B(u(\tau)),
$$

with

$$
K=\zeta\zeta_\tau^2,
\qquad
H=\zeta_\tau^{-1/2}W,
$$

A3 gives

$$
W_{\zeta\zeta}+\zeta W=\Psi_B(\zeta)W,
$$

where away from the turning point

$$
\Psi_B(\zeta)
=
\frac{\zeta K_{\tau\tau}}{4K^2}
-
\frac{5\zeta K_\tau^2}{16K^3}
+
\frac{5}{16\zeta^2}.
$$

At a simple turning point,

$$
\Psi_B(0)
=
\frac{
10\gamma K_{\tau\tau\tau}(\tau_0)
-
9K_{\tau\tau}(\tau_0)^2
}{
140\gamma^{8/3}
},
\qquad
\gamma=K_\tau(\tau_0)>0.
$$

A3's hand cancellation is credible. It should be entered as "hand-certified; CAS log pending," not as a fully archival computer-verified formula.

## A3

1. The removable Langer value is credible, but the stated "complete hand derivation" should be converted into an archival CAS or line-by-line algebra log before being treated as permanent.

2. The gamma-ratio envelope is not certified. In particular, A3's stated Wendel inequality

$$
\frac{\Gamma(x+\alpha)}{\Gamma(x)}
\le
(x+\alpha-1)^\alpha
\qquad
(0<\alpha\le1,\ x\ge1)
$$

is false as written. For example, at $x=1$, $\alpha=1/2$ it would give

$$
\Gamma(3/2)\le 2^{-1/2},
$$

but

$$
\Gamma(3/2)=\frac{\sqrt\pi}{2}>2^{-1/2}.
$$

The standard Wendel inequality has a different form; one common version for $0<s<1$ is

$$
\left(\frac{x}{x+s}\right)^{1-s}
\le
\frac{\Gamma(x+s)}{x^s\Gamma(x)}
\le
1
\qquad
(x>0),
$$

so care is required. The gamma-ratio part of A3's output must be downgraded from certified to "attempt; inequality statement needs repair."

3. The claim that $u_\sigma\le n$ has equality only when $n=1$ and $B\to\infty$ is inaccurate. For $n=1$,

$$
u_\sigma=\frac{B}{B}=1
$$

for every admissible $B$. This is minor but should be corrected in the lemma-bank wording.

4. A3's "L-Wendel1 certified" status should be removed until the exact gamma inequality and parameter substitutions are written correctly.

5. The Riccati Taylor data need a remainder bound and a proof that the Riccati quotient does not hit a zero of $H$ before the intended endpoint.

Possible errors or hidden assumptions:

## A2 hidden assumptions

1. **Circular zero-safety.** A2's relative-amplitude variable $h=Y/W_1$ is only meaningful while $W_1>0$. The phase-shift proof tries to show $W_1(z_1^*)>0$, but some intermediate estimates appear to assume the same fact.

2. **Monotonicity beyond the reference peak.** The clean integral bound using monotonicity of $J_\alpha$ is valid only before $j'_{\alpha,1}$. A2's tail estimate beyond the peak requires a new bound, not the same monotonicity argument.

3. **Taylor remainder.** The phase-shift formula uses a linear expansion at $T_0=j'_{\alpha,1}$. The second-order remainder in $J_\alpha'$ can be harmless, but this must be proved with a displayed bound.

4. **Unproved Bessel zero constants.** Constants such as $1.05\alpha^{1/3}$ for $j_{\alpha,1}-j'_{\alpha,1}$ are asymptotic references, not certified inequalities over the required $\alpha\ge1/2$ range.

5. **Gamma margin.** The proposed inequality has narrow margin after exponentiation. A small gamma-normalization error can destroy the threshold. A2 correctly names this dependency but should not claim a regime theorem before it is supplied.

6. **Airy sign overclaim.** A leading-form calculation for $\Psi_B(0)$ does not prove global positivity or monotone relative growth.

## A3 hidden assumptions

1. **External gamma inequality misstatement.** The Wendel/Gautschi/Kershaw layer is not yet safe. Any proof depending on the typed Wendel form is invalid until repaired.

2. **CAS-free Langer residual.** Hand cancellation can be accepted provisionally, but the project needs a reproducible expansion log because this formula is convention-sensitive.

3. **Expanded $n=2$ coefficients.** The factored equation is safer than the expanded cubic. The expanded form is consistent with direct expansion after a specific denominator-clearing factor, but implementation should use the factored formula unless the convention is frozen.

4. **Endpoint equality wording.** The $n=1$ cap equality should be stated exactly.
