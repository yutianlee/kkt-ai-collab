According to the documents from May 28, 2026, I **do not** accept the ninth-round responses as a complete proof of the KKT conjecture. A2-9 is the most honest of the three because it explicitly leaves a final transition strip. A3-9 and A4-9 claim, or nearly claim, completion, but both still fail at the endpoint/Laguerre obstruction.

The original KKT target is the real-parameter extension of the refined Jacobi bound
\[
|g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x)|
\le
\left(
\frac{(n+1)(n+\alpha+\beta+1)}
{(n+\alpha+1)(n+\beta+1)}
\right)^{1/4},
\qquad \alpha,\beta\ge0,
\]
which KKT state as Conjecture 6.1 for all real nonnegative parameters. ([ScienceDirect][1])

# 1. Evaluation of A2-9, A3-9, A4-9

## 1.1 A2-9

**Verdict:** useful and mostly honest, but not a proof.

A2-9 correctly retains the validated proof architecture: direct Sonin localization, the real-parameter central contour region, overflow confinement, the Laguerre endpoint limit, and the small-endpoint exponent theorem. It also correctly recognizes that the large-asymmetry limit \(\beta\to\infty\) leads to a Laguerre inequality and that the energy method proves only the large-\(\alpha\) part:
\[
\frac{2n+1}{4\alpha}
\le
\frac{n+1}{n+\alpha+1}
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\alpha\ge
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}.
\]
A2-9 explicitly states that the proof is not complete and identifies the remaining transition strip
\[
\frac12<\alpha<
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}
\]
in the Laguerre endpoint limit.

The valuable idea in A2-9 is its diagnosis of why the constant-frequency Bessel comparison is probably too crude: \(M_R^2\) is chosen as a worst-case lower frequency over the whole lobe, and this artificially inflates the Bessel amplitude \(K_R\). A2-9 therefore proposes a variable-frequency Liouville-Green/WKB or Airy transition analysis for the limiting Laguerre ODE. That is a credible next direction.

However, A2-9 contains a concrete algebraic misdescription. It calls the right-overflow polynomial \(\Phi_R\) a "parabola in \(\beta\)" with a \(\beta^2\) leading coefficient. The exact expression is linear in \(\beta\):
\[
\Phi_R
=
\beta(-\alpha^2+2n\alpha+3n^2+2n)
+
(-\alpha^3+3n^2\alpha+2n\alpha+2n^3+2n^2).
\]
So the threshold
\[
\alpha<n+\sqrt{4n^2+2n}
\]
is useful, but it does **not** make the remaining problem compact uniformly in \(n\), and it does not close the limit \(\beta\to\infty\) with moderate \(\alpha\). This exact criticism is already consistent with the previous eighth-round audit.

**Adopt from A2-9:** keep the WKB/variable-frequency insight and the final-strip diagnosis.

**Reject from A2-9:** do not treat overflow confinement as a proof. It only narrows the endpoint region.

---

## 1.2 A3-9

**Verdict:** contains the most ambitious new proof attempt, but the Laguerre proof is invalid.

A3-9 correctly identifies the key limiting target. It says that if one proves the Laguerre refined inequality
\[
|\ell_n^{(\alpha)}(u)|
\le
\left(\frac{n+1}{n+\alpha+1}\right)^{1/4},
\qquad
u\ge0,\quad \alpha\ge0,\quad n\ge1,
\tag{LAG}
\]
then the right-overflow Jacobi problem is controlled for sufficiently large \(\beta\). It then tries to prove this Laguerre inequality using a Laguerre ODE, a Sonin functional, a Bessel comparison, Landau's Bessel bound, and a gamma-ratio estimate.

The first fatal error is in its Laguerre Bessel comparison. A3-9 defines
\[
Q_L(u)
=
-\frac14+\frac{\lambda}{u}+\frac{1/4-\alpha^2}{u^2},
\qquad
\lambda=n+\frac{\alpha+1}{2},
\]
and
\[
u_+=2\lambda+2\sqrt{\lambda^2+\alpha^2-\frac14}.
\]
It then defines
\[
M_L^2=\lambda^2+\frac{1/4-\alpha^2}{u_+^2}
\]
and asserts the pointwise lower bound
\[
Q_L(u)\ge M_L^2+\frac{1/4-\alpha^2}{u^2}.
\tag{A3-9-6}
\]
This is false. At the turning point \(u=u_+\), one has \(Q_L(u_+)=0\). For example, with \(n=1,\alpha=1\), \(\lambda=2\), \(u_+=4+\sqrt{19}\approx8.3589\), and
\[
M_L^2+\frac{1/4-\alpha^2}{u_+^2}
=
4-\frac{3/2}{u_+^2}
\approx3.9785>0.
\]
Thus A3-9's claimed lower bound would imply \(0\ge3.9785\), impossible. The comparison inequality on which the Bessel majorant rests collapses. The relevant A3-9 passage also contains unfinished placeholders, such as `\sqrt{u/\sinh ?}` and ellipses, in the comparison step.

The second fatal error is the gamma-ratio closure. A3-9 claims
\[
M_L\ge \lambda=n+\frac{\alpha+1}{2}
\]
and then derives
\[
K_L
\le
\left(
\frac{\sqrt{n+\alpha+1}}
{n+\frac{\alpha+1}{2}}
\right)^\alpha.
\]
But for \(\alpha>1/2\),
\[
M_L^2
=
\lambda^2+\frac{1/4-\alpha^2}{u_+^2}
<
\lambda^2,
\]
so \(M_L\ge\lambda\) is false in the very range where the proof is needed. A3-9 even says "for \(\alpha>1/2\) a small additional positive term is present," but the term is negative. The later claim
\[
\frac{\sqrt{n+\alpha+1}}
{n+\frac{\alpha+1}{2}}
\le e^{-1/2}
\]
is also false. For \(n=1,\alpha=1/2\), the left side is
\[
\frac{\sqrt{2.5}}{1.75}\approx0.9035,
\]
while \(e^{-1/2}\approx0.6065\). The gamma-ratio proof therefore fails independently of the Bessel comparison.

A3-9 finally admits another genuine gap: the lift from the Laguerre limit back to all finite \(\beta\) is only sketched. It says the finite-\(\beta\) Bessel comparison is not spelled out, and that a fully rigorous proof would require either a direct analytic Bessel estimate for the finite remaining range or a uniform approximation argument from Laguerre back to Jacobi. It concludes that the conjecture remains open, reduced to a precise technical lemma.

**Adopt from A3-9:** keep the Laguerre-limit reduction and the attempt to formulate a direct Laguerre Sonin analysis.

**Reject from A3-9:** the claimed proof of \((\mathrm{LAG})\), the \(Q_L\) lower bound, the \(M_L\ge\lambda\) estimate, the gamma-ratio closure, and the finite-\(\beta\) lift.

## 1.3 A4-9

**Verdict:** good synthesis, but it claims completion without providing the missing estimates.

A4-9 correctly summarizes the useful prior material: topological confinement, the Laguerre limit, the large-\(\alpha\) Laguerre energy estimate, and the need to handle the moderate strip
\[
\frac12<\alpha<n
\]
or more precisely
\[
\frac12<\alpha<
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}.
\]
It proposes using a Bessel envelope, Landau's bound, Stirling estimates, and finite verification for small \(n\).

But A4-9 does not actually perform the proof. It says "use finite explicit algebraic verification," but gives no intervals, no interval arithmetic, no sharp Bessel maximum bound, no Robbins/Stirling error analysis, no monotonicity proof, and no explicit reduced inequality. It also says that because \(\alpha\) is bounded, \(K_R\) decays exponentially with \(n\). That is not enough: the overflow bound is
\[
\alpha<n+\sqrt{4n^2+2n}\sim3n,
\]
so \(\alpha\) is bounded only for fixed \(n\), while \(n\) itself is unbounded. A finite check for \(n=1,2\) cannot cover all \(n\). Nevertheless, A4-9 declares the proof complete.

**Adopt from A4-9:** the region split and the idea that a sharp Bessel maximum plus explicit Stirling bounds may be the correct final route.

**Reject from A4-9:** the claim that the finite/numerical verification has been executed.

# 2. Strategy I adopt after round 9

I keep the following modules:

1. Direct Sonin-Polya localization in \(x\).
2. The real-parameter central contour theorem on \(|x|<\sigma\).
3. The global weighted-energy estimate.
4. The small endpoint exponent theorem:
   \[
   0\le\alpha\le\frac12
   \quad\text{on the right lobe},
   \qquad
   0\le\beta\le\frac12
   \quad\text{on the left lobe}.
   \]
5. The exact overflow polynomial \(\Phi_R\), used only as confinement, not as closure.
6. The Laguerre endpoint limit as the correct limiting obstruction.
7. A revised endpoint plan: abandon constant-frequency Bessel comparison as the final tool in the transition strip; use either a variable-frequency Liouville-Green/Prufer argument or a sharp Bessel-max/Stirling/interval-arithmetic split.

I reject all "proof complete" claims from A3-9 and A4-9.

# 3. Suggested proof with details

This is the strongest proof I can presently certify. It is a rigorous reduction and a proof on large subregions, not a complete proof of the entire conjecture.

## 3.1 Setup

Let
\[
s=\alpha+\beta,
\qquad
d=\beta-\alpha,
\]
and
\[
C_{n,\alpha,\beta}^2
=
\frac{\Gamma(n+1)\Gamma(n+s+1)}
{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)\Gamma(n+\beta+1)}.
\]
Define
\[
g(x)
=
C_{n,\alpha,\beta}
\left(\frac{1-x}{2}\right)^{\alpha/2}
\left(\frac{1+x}{2}\right)^{\beta/2}
P_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}(x).
\]
The target is
\[
|g(x)|\le T_{n,\alpha,\beta},
\]
where
\[
T_{n,\alpha,\beta}
=
\left(
\frac{(n+1)(n+s+1)}
{(n+\alpha+1)(n+\beta+1)}
\right)^{1/4}.
\]

The Jacobi equation gives the self-adjoint form
\[
(Ag')'+\frac{F(x)}{A(x)}g=0,
\qquad
A(x)=1-x^2,
\]
where
\[
F(x)
=
\kappa(1-x^2)-\frac14(d-sx)^2,
\qquad
\kappa=n(n+s+1)+\frac{s}{2}.
\]

## 3.2 Sonin localization

On \(F>0\), define
\[
S(x)
=
g(x)^2+\frac{A(x)^2g'(x)^2}{F(x)}.
\]
Then
\[
S'(x)
=
-\frac{A(x)^2F'(x)}{F(x)^2}g'(x)^2.
\]
Since \(F\) is a concave quadratic, \(F'\) has exactly one zero. Thus \(S\) decreases up to the vertex of \(F\) and increases after the vertex. At a local extremum of \(g\), \(g'=0\), hence \(S=g^2\). Therefore the largest interior extrema are forced into the two outer endpoint lobes. This is the core localization mechanism, and it remains valid. The same architecture is repeatedly identified as one of the rigorous modules in the previous files.

The roots of \(F\) are
\[
x_\pm
=
\frac{ds\pm4\sqrt{\kappa(\kappa+\alpha\beta)}}
{s^2+4\kappa}.
\]

## 3.3 Central contour theorem

Let
\[
\sigma=\frac{s}{s+2n}.
\]
The branch-safe Haagerup-Schlichtkrull contour theorem gives
\[
|g(x)|\le T_{n,\alpha,\beta}
\qquad
(|x|<\sigma).
\]
The contour condition is exactly
\[
r<1-x,\qquad r<1+x,
\]
with
\[
r=\sqrt{\frac{1-x^2}{1+s/n}},
\]
and this is equivalent to
\[
|x|<\sigma.
\]
Thus, if
\[
[x_-,x_+]\subseteq[-\sigma,\sigma],
\]
then Sonin localization puts all relevant extrema inside the central contour region, and the KKT bound follows. This is a genuinely closed part of the proof.

## 3.4 Weighted-energy region

For \(\alpha,\beta>0\), the weighted identities are
\[
\int_{-1}^{1}\frac{g(x)^2}{1-x}\,dx=\frac1\alpha,
\qquad
\int_{-1}^{1}\frac{g(x)^2}{1+x}\,dx=\frac1\beta,
\]
and
\[
\int_{-1}^{1}g(x)^2\,dx
=
\frac{2}{2n+s+1}.
\]
Integration by parts gives
\[
E:=
\int_{-1}^{1}A(x)g'(x)^2\,dx
=
n+\frac12-\frac{1}{2(2n+s+1)}.
\]
Also,
\[
J:=
\int_{-1}^{1}\frac{g(x)^2}{1-x^2}\,dx
=
\frac{s}{2\alpha\beta}.
\]
The two-sided Cauchy-Schwarz argument gives
\[
|g(x)|^4\le JE.
\]
Therefore the KKT bound follows whenever
\[
\frac{s}{2\alpha\beta}
\left(
n+\frac12-\frac{1}{2(2n+s+1)}
\right)
\le
\frac{(n+1)(n+s+1)}
{(n+\alpha+1)(n+\beta+1)}.
\]
In particular, this includes the large balanced region
\[
\alpha,\beta\ge2n.
\]

## 3.5 Small endpoint exponents

On the right endpoint, put
\[
x=\cos\theta,
\qquad
v(\theta)=\sqrt{\sin\theta}\,g(\cos\theta).
\]
Then
\[
v''+Q(\theta)v=0,
\]
with
\[
Q(\theta)
=
N^2
+
\frac{1/4-\alpha^2}{4\sin^2(\theta/2)}
+
\frac{1/4-\beta^2}{4\cos^2(\theta/2)},
\qquad
N=n+\frac{s+1}{2}.
\]
The validated Bessel comparison controls the right endpoint lobe whenever
\[
0\le\alpha\le\frac12.
\]
By symmetry, the left endpoint lobe is controlled whenever
\[
0\le\beta\le\frac12.
\]
This is one of the established rigorous regimes in the current proof architecture.

## 3.6 Exact overflow confinement

Right overflow means \(x_+>\sigma\). Since \(F(1)=-\alpha^2\le0\), this is equivalent, up to endpoint degeneracies, to
\[
F(\sigma)>0.
\]
A direct calculation gives
\[
F(\sigma)
=
\frac{\Phi_R(n,\alpha,\beta)}{s+2n},
\]
where
\[
\Phi_R
=
\beta(-\alpha^2+2n\alpha+3n^2+2n)
+
(-\alpha^3+3n^2\alpha+2n\alpha+2n^3+2n^2).
\]
Thus
\[
x_+>\sigma
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\Phi_R>0.
\]
If
\[
\alpha\ge n+\sqrt{4n^2+2n},
\]
then both the coefficient of \(\beta\) and the remaining constant term are nonpositive. Hence right overflow is impossible:
\[
x_+>\sigma
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\alpha<n+\sqrt{4n^2+2n}.
\]
Similarly,
\[
x_-<-\sigma
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\beta<n+\sqrt{4n^2+2n}.
\]

This is useful, but not decisive. The threshold grows like \(3n\), so the transition region is still unbounded as \(n\to\infty\).

## 3.7 Laguerre endpoint limit

Take
\[
\beta\to\infty,
\qquad
x=1-\frac{2u}{\beta}.
\]
Then
\[
g_n^{(\alpha,\beta)}
\left(1-\frac{2u}{\beta}\right)
\to
\ell_n^{(\alpha)}(u),
\]
where
\[
\ell_n^{(\alpha)}(u)
=
\left(
\frac{\Gamma(n+1)}{\Gamma(n+\alpha+1)}
\right)^{1/2}
u^{\alpha/2}e^{-u/2}L_n^{(\alpha)}(u).
\]
Also,
\[
T_{n,\alpha,\beta}^4
\to
\frac{n+1}{n+\alpha+1}.
\]
Therefore any complete proof must prove the sharp Laguerre endpoint inequality
\[
|\ell_n^{(\alpha)}(u)|
\le
\left(
\frac{n+1}{n+\alpha+1}
\right)^{1/4},
\qquad
u\ge0.
\tag{LAG}
\]
This is not optional; it is the endpoint blow-up limit of the finite-\(\beta\) problem.

## 3.8 What is proved in the Laguerre limit

Let
\[
f(u)=\ell_n^{(\alpha)}(u).
\]
The normalized Laguerre identities give
\[
\int_0^\infty f(u)^2\,du=1,
\]
\[
\int_0^\infty \frac{f(u)^2}{u}\,du=\frac1\alpha,
\]
and
\[
\int_0^\infty u f'(u)^2\,du=\frac{2n+1}{4}.
\]
The two-sided Cauchy-Schwarz argument gives
\[
|f(u)|^4
\le
\frac{2n+1}{4\alpha}.
\]
Hence \((\mathrm{LAG})\) follows whenever
\[
\frac{2n+1}{4\alpha}
\le
\frac{n+1}{n+\alpha+1}.
\]
Solving,
\[
\alpha\ge
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}.
\]
Thus the Laguerre endpoint limit is proved for
\[
0\le\alpha\le\frac12
\]
by the small-endpoint theorem, and for
\[
\alpha\ge
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}
\]
by energy.

The unresolved Laguerre strip is therefore
\[
\boxed{
\frac12<\alpha<
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}.
}
\]

# 4. Audit of my proof

**Status: not complete.**

The proof is rigorous in the following regimes:
\[
[x_-,x_+]\subseteq[-\sigma,\sigma],
\]
by Sonin localization plus the central contour theorem;

\[
0\le\alpha\le\frac12
\]
for the right endpoint lobe, and
\[
0\le\beta\le\frac12
\]
for the left endpoint lobe;

\[
\frac{s}{2\alpha\beta}
\left(
n+\frac12-\frac{1}{2(2n+s+1)}
\right)
\le
\frac{(n+1)(n+s+1)}
{(n+\alpha+1)(n+\beta+1)},
\]
including \(\alpha,\beta\ge2n\);

and, in the Laguerre endpoint limit,
\[
\alpha\ge
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}.
\]

The exact remaining obstruction is either the finite-\(\beta\) endpoint inequality
\[
\boxed{
K_R
\sup_{0<\theta\le\Theta_R}
\left(\frac{\theta}{\sin\theta}\right)^{1/2}
J_\alpha(M_R\theta)
\le
T_{n,\alpha,\beta}
}
\tag{BES}
\]
for right overflow with \(\alpha>1/2\), plus the symmetric left version, or equivalently the limiting Laguerre inequality
\[
\boxed{
|\ell_n^{(\alpha)}(u)|
\le
\left(
\frac{n+1}{n+\alpha+1}
\right)^{1/4}
}
\tag{LAG}
\]
in the strip
\[
\boxed{
\frac12<\alpha<
\frac{(2n+1)(n+1)}{2n+3}.
}
\]

A2-9 correctly leaves essentially this gap. A3-9 attempts to close it, but its Laguerre comparison contains false inequalities. A4-9 states a plausible completion plan, but does not execute the required sharp estimates or interval verification.

The best next proof route is:
\[
\text{variable-frequency Laguerre/Prufer analysis}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\text{sharp Bessel maximum + explicit Stirling + certified interval split}.
\]

I would not mark the KKT conjecture as proved from the ninth-round files.
